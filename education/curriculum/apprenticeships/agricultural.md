# Agricultural Apprenticeship — 2-Year Intensive Program

## Overview
The agricultural apprenticeship produces community food system managers capable of planning, implementing, and managing crop and livestock production, food processing, and agricultural economics. Graduates can independently manage a farm operation that feeds the community.

**Duration**: 2 years (alongside Grade 11-12 coursework)
**Prerequisites**: Completion of advanced agriculture science through Grade 10, demonstrated aptitude in farm work
**Outcome**: Community agricultural manager capable of independent farm operations

---

## Year 1: Production Systems

### Module 1: Advanced Crop Production (Months 1-4)
**Duration**: 16 weeks (ideally beginning with growing season)
**Materials**: Agricultural land, seeds, soil testing equipment, irrigation supplies, tools
**Survival Relevance**: Crop production is the foundation of community food security

#### Learning Objectives
- Manage crop production from soil preparation through harvest
- Apply soil science to optimize fertility and structure
- Implement integrated pest management at the farm scale
- Plan crop rotations for sustained productivity

#### Content
- Soil management: testing, amendment, fertility planning, cover cropping, composting at scale
- Crop planning: variety selection, planting schedules, succession planting, companion planting
- Seed management: seed saving protocols, germination testing, variety trials, breeding basics
- Irrigation management: system design, scheduling, efficiency optimization, drought response
- Pest and disease management: scouting protocols, identification, biological controls, cultural controls, threshold-based intervention
- Weed management: prevention, cultivation, mulching, competitive planting
- Harvest management: timing, technique, handling, post-harvest treatment
- Season extension: cold frames, row covers, greenhouse management, succession planting
- Record keeping: yield records, input tracking, labor logs, weather data

#### Assessment
- Manage a production plot through a complete growing season
- Maintain soil fertility records and amendment plans
- Implement IPM with documented scouting and decision-making
- Achieve target yields for assigned crops

---

### Module 2: Livestock Management (Months 5-8)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Livestock, feed, housing, veterinary supplies, breeding records
**Survival Relevance**: Livestock provide protein, labor, fertilizer, and materials — managing them well maximizes community nutrition

#### Learning Objectives
- Manage livestock herds/flocks for production and health
- Formulate balanced rations from available feeds
- Implement breeding programs for genetic improvement
- Provide basic veterinary care

#### Content
- Poultry management: flock health, egg production optimization, meat bird management, incubation, breeding
- Small ruminants (goats/sheep): feeding, health, milking, breeding, kidding/lambing, hoof care
- Large livestock (cattle if available): feeding, health, breeding, calving, draft animal training
- Swine: feeding, health, farrowing, growth management
- Nutrition science: ration formulation, feed analysis, supplementation, feed production (forage, hay, silage)
- Breeding program management: selection criteria, pedigree tracking, inbreeding avoidance, performance records
- Veterinary skills: health assessment, vaccination, deworming, wound care, hoof trimming, birthing assistance, castration, common disease treatment
- Housing design: ventilation, space requirements, waste management, predator protection
- Pasture management: rotational grazing, carrying capacity, pasture renovation

#### Assessment
- Manage a livestock operation for 4 months with production records
- Formulate balanced rations for 3 species
- Maintain breeding records and make selection decisions
- Perform veterinary procedures under supervision

---

### Module 3: Aquaculture and Integrated Systems (Months 9-11)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Pond or tank access, water testing equipment, fish stock, feed, aquaponics components
**Survival Relevance**: Fish are the most feed-efficient protein source. Integrated systems maximize production per unit of land and water.

#### Learning Objectives
- Manage aquaculture production systems
- Maintain water quality for fish health and growth
- Design integrated systems (aquaponics, rice-fish, pond-garden)
- Calculate production efficiency and economic viability

#### Content
- Aquaculture systems: pond culture, tank culture, raceway — design, construction, management
- Species management: stocking density, feeding programs, growth monitoring, harvest
- Water quality: dissolved oxygen, ammonia, pH, temperature — monitoring and management
- Disease management: prevention, recognition, treatment
- Aquaponics: design, fish-plant balance, nutrient cycling, system management
- Integrated systems: rice-fish culture, pond-garden integration, multi-trophic systems
- Processing: harvest methods, cleaning, preservation (smoking, drying, salting)
- Economics: feed conversion ratios, production costs, break-even analysis

#### Assessment
- Manage an aquaculture system for 3 months with daily monitoring
- Maintain water quality within target parameters
- Design an integrated production system
- Calculate production economics

---

### Module 4: Food Processing and Preservation (Months 11-12)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Processing equipment, preservation supplies, kitchen/processing facility
**Survival Relevance**: Food production is meaningless without preservation. 40%+ of food can be lost without proper processing and storage.

#### Learning Objectives
- Process and preserve food using 5+ methods
- Manage food safety throughout the processing chain
- Design processing workflows for peak harvest volumes
- Calculate storage requirements and shelf life

#### Content
- Drying: solar dehydration, smoke drying, air drying — for fruits, vegetables, herbs, meat, fish
- Fermentation: lacto-fermentation (sauerkraut, kimchi, pickles), vinegar, yogurt, cheese basics, sourdough
- Smoking: cold smoking (preservation), hot smoking (cooking + preservation), smokehouse design and management
- Salting and curing: dry salt, brine, sugar curing — for meat, fish, vegetables
- Canning (if equipment available): water bath (high-acid foods), pressure canning (low-acid foods), safety protocols
- Root cellaring: storage conditions by crop, monitoring, loss prevention
- Food safety: contamination prevention, temperature management, sanitation, testing
- Processing facility design: workflow, equipment layout, sanitation, capacity planning
- Storage management: rotation systems, inventory tracking, loss monitoring

#### Assessment
- Process and preserve food using 5 different methods
- Maintain food safety standards throughout processing
- Design a processing workflow for a peak harvest scenario
- Manage a storage facility with documented inventory

## Year 2: Farm Management and Capstone

### Module 5: Permaculture Design and Farm Planning (Months 13-16)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Design materials, community agricultural land, reference materials
**Survival Relevance**: Permaculture designs self-sustaining food systems that require less labor and fewer inputs over time

#### Content
- Permaculture design: ethics, principles, zone and sector analysis, pattern design
- Farm layout: integrating crops, livestock, water, energy, structures, and processing
- Water management: watershed analysis, rainwater harvesting, swales, irrigation, drainage
- Soil building at scale: composting systems, cover cropping programs, amendment strategies
- Agroforestry: food forests, silvopasture, alley cropping — design and establishment
- Farm economics: enterprise budgets, labor planning, capital investment, 5-year projections
- Climate adaptation: variety selection, season extension, drought/flood preparedness

#### Assessment
- Create a complete farm design with all elements integrated
- Design a water management system with hydraulic calculations
- Produce a 5-year farm economic plan
- Present farm design to community agricultural leadership

---

### Module 6: Agricultural Economics and Management (Months 17-19)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Community agricultural data, economic planning tools
**Survival Relevance**: Efficient management determines whether a farm feeds the community or wastes resources

#### Content
- Production planning: yield targets, input budgets, labor schedules, seasonal calendars
- Record keeping and analysis: production records, financial records, performance analysis
- Labor management: task assignment, training, safety, efficiency
- Equipment management: maintenance schedules, repair, replacement planning
- Trade and marketing: surplus allocation, trade negotiation, inter-community agricultural agreements
- Risk management: crop failure contingency, pest outbreak response, weather event planning
- Community nutrition planning: caloric needs, dietary diversity, seasonal food availability, storage planning

#### Assessment
- Create a complete production plan for the community farm
- Maintain financial records for a farm operation
- Develop a community nutrition plan with seasonal food calendar
- Present risk management plan

---

### Module 7: Capstone — Manage a Production System (Months 20-24)
**Duration**: 5 months (full growing season)
**Materials**: Community agricultural resources
**Survival Relevance**: The capstone demonstrates the apprentice can actually feed people

#### Structure
Each apprentice takes complete responsibility for a significant production unit:
- A diversified garden plot (1+ acre)
- A livestock operation (poultry flock, small ruminant herd, or swine)
- An aquaculture system
- A food processing and preservation operation
- An orchard/perennial system establishment

#### Requirements
- Design the system with written justification
- Implement from planning through establishment
- Manage daily operations for the growing/production season
- Maintain complete records (inputs, outputs, labor, financial, observations)
- Harvest, process, and preserve products
- Calculate production efficiency and economic viability
- Write a comprehensive season report with analysis and recommendations
- Present results to community agricultural leadership
- Teaching: deliver 5 agricultural lessons to younger students
- Mentor at least 2 intermediate band students in agricultural skills

#### Assessment
- Production system produces usable food for the community
- Complete records demonstrating competent management
- Season report with production analysis and recommendations
- Teaching delivered to younger students
- Community agricultural leadership certifies readiness for independent farm management
