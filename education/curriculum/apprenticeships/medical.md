# Medical Apprenticeship — 2-Year Intensive Program

## Overview
The medical apprenticeship produces community healthcare providers capable of managing common illnesses, trauma, preventive medicine, pharmacy operations, and public health. This is the most demanding apprenticeship track, combining rigorous academic study with extensive supervised clinical practice.

**Duration**: 2 years (alongside Grade 11-12 coursework)
**Prerequisites**: Completion of advanced biology through Grade 10, advanced medicine through Grade 10, demonstrated aptitude and commitment
**Outcome**: Community healthcare provider capable of independent practice under protocol

---

## Year 1: Clinical Foundations

### Module 1: Advanced Anatomy and Patient Assessment (Months 1-3)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Anatomy references, assessment equipment (stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, thermometer, otoscope), practice patients
**Survival Relevance**: Accurate assessment is the foundation of all medical care — wrong diagnosis leads to wrong treatment

#### Learning Objectives
- Perform a complete head-to-toe physical examination
- Take a comprehensive patient history using SAMPLE and OPQRST frameworks
- Identify normal vs abnormal findings for all body systems
- Document assessments accurately in medical records

#### Content
- Systematic physical examination technique
- Vital signs: blood pressure, pulse, respiration, temperature, oxygen saturation (if pulse ox available)
- SAMPLE history: Signs/Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past medical history, Last oral intake, Events
- OPQRST pain assessment: Onset, Provoke/Palliate, Quality, Region/Radiation, Severity, Time
- Cardiovascular assessment: heart sounds, pulse quality, peripheral circulation, edema
- Respiratory assessment: lung sounds, breathing patterns, chest movement, cough character
- Abdominal assessment: inspection, auscultation, palpation (light and deep), organ assessment
- Neurological assessment: mental status, cranial nerves, motor function, sensation, reflexes
- Musculoskeletal assessment: range of motion, strength, joint stability, deformity
- Documentation: SOAP notes (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan)

#### Assessment
- Perform a complete physical examination in under 30 minutes
- Take a comprehensive history and identify key findings
- Document 20 patient encounters using SOAP format

---

### Module 2: Pathophysiology and Diagnosis (Months 4-6)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Medical references, case studies, diagnostic equipment
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding disease mechanisms enables accurate diagnosis even without laboratory support

#### Learning Objectives
- Explain the pathophysiology of 50+ common conditions
- Develop differential diagnoses based on clinical findings
- Identify conditions requiring urgent intervention
- Recognize when a condition exceeds community treatment capability

#### Content
**Infectious Disease**: Bacterial infections (skin, respiratory, urinary, gastrointestinal), viral infections (respiratory, gastrointestinal, systemic), parasitic infections (intestinal, skin, systemic), fungal infections. Recognition, differentiation, and management.

**Cardiovascular**: Hypertension, heart failure, arrhythmias, chest pain evaluation, peripheral vascular disease, deep vein thrombosis. Assessment and management within community capability.

**Respiratory**: Pneumonia, asthma/COPD, bronchitis, tuberculosis recognition, upper respiratory infections. Differentiation and management.

**Gastrointestinal**: Appendicitis recognition, bowel obstruction, gastroenteritis, peptic ulcer, hepatitis, dehydration management.

**Musculoskeletal**: Fractures, dislocations, sprains, strains, back pain, arthritis, overuse injuries.

**Endocrine**: Diabetes recognition and management, thyroid disorders, dehydration, electrolyte imbalance.

**Mental Health**: Depression, anxiety, PTSD, grief, psychosis recognition, substance use disorders, suicide assessment.

#### Assessment
- Develop differential diagnoses for 30 case presentations
- Identify urgent conditions requiring immediate intervention
- Pass written examination on pathophysiology of common conditions

---

### Module 3: Pharmacology and Pharmacy Management (Months 7-9)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Pharmacology references, community pharmacy inventory, dosing calculators
**Survival Relevance**: Correct medication use saves lives; incorrect use kills. Pharmacy management ensures medications are available when needed.

#### Learning Objectives
- Explain mechanism of action for 40+ medications
- Calculate dosages for adults and children
- Manage a community pharmacy (inventory, storage, dispensing, record-keeping)
- Identify drug interactions and contraindications
- Prepare basic compounded medications

#### Content
- Drug classes: antibiotics, analgesics, anti-inflammatories, antihypertensives, cardiac medications, respiratory medications, gastrointestinal medications, psychiatric medications, hormonal medications, topical treatments
- Dosing: weight-based calculation, pediatric dosing, renal/hepatic adjustment principles
- Administration routes: oral, topical, intramuscular, subcutaneous, rectal, ophthalmic
- Drug interactions and contraindications
- Pharmacy operations: inventory management, expiration tracking, storage conditions, dispensing protocols, record-keeping
- Basic compounding: solutions, suspensions, ointments, suppositories from available ingredients
- Natural medicine integration: herbal preparations with evidence base, interactions with pharmaceuticals

#### Assessment
- Calculate correct dosages for 50 clinical scenarios
- Manage pharmacy inventory for 1 month with zero dispensing errors
- Prepare 5 compounded medications to standard
- Pass pharmacology written examination

---

### Module 4: Emergency Medicine and Trauma (Months 10-12)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Trauma training equipment, suture kits, splinting materials, airway management equipment
**Survival Relevance**: Trauma and medical emergencies are the situations where competent care most directly saves lives

#### Learning Objectives
- Manage trauma patients using systematic assessment (ABCDE)
- Perform emergency procedures: hemorrhage control, airway management, chest decompression principles
- Manage common medical emergencies: anaphylaxis, seizures, diabetic emergencies, cardiac events
- Lead a medical response team during mass casualty events

#### Content
- Primary survey: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure
- Hemorrhage control: direct pressure, wound packing, tourniquet application, hemostatic agents
- Airway management: positioning, suctioning, oropharyngeal airway, bag-valve mask
- Wound management: cleaning, debridement, closure (suture, staple, tape), dressing
- Fracture management: assessment, reduction principles, splinting, casting
- Burns: classification, fluid management, wound care, pain management
- Medical emergencies: anaphylaxis (epinephrine), seizures, diabetic emergencies, chest pain, stroke recognition
- Triage: START triage system, resource allocation during mass casualty

#### Assessment
- Manage 10 simulated trauma scenarios following ABCDE protocol
- Demonstrate all emergency procedures to competency standard
- Lead a simulated mass casualty response
- Pass practical skills examination

## Year 2: Advanced Practice and Clinical Rotation

### Module 5: Procedural Skills (Months 13-16)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Suture kits, splinting materials, IV supplies (training), obstetric training models, dental instruments
**Survival Relevance**: These procedures address the most critical gaps in community healthcare capability

#### Content
- Advanced wound management: complex laceration repair, abscess drainage, foreign body removal, skin grafting principles
- Orthopedic procedures: fracture reduction, joint relocation, casting and splinting advanced
- IV therapy: access principles, fluid selection, rate calculation (theory and simulation)
- Obstetric care: prenatal assessment, normal delivery management, complication recognition, postpartum care, newborn assessment
- Dental procedures: emergency extraction, abscess management, temporary restoration, pain management
- Minor surgical procedures: incision and drainage, ingrown toenail, cyst removal, wound debridement
- Anesthesia basics: local anesthesia (lidocaine), regional blocks, pain management protocols

#### Assessment
- Demonstrate each procedural skill to competency standard
- Manage 5 simulated obstetric scenarios
- Perform dental emergency procedures on models
- Pass comprehensive practical examination

---

### Module 6: Public Health and Preventive Medicine (Months 17-19)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Community health data, epidemiological tools, water testing equipment
**Survival Relevance**: Prevention is more effective than treatment. Public health protects the entire community.

#### Content
- Epidemiology: disease surveillance, outbreak investigation, contact tracing
- Water and sanitation: water quality testing, treatment methods, sanitation system maintenance
- Immunization: principles, schedule management, cold chain (if available), adverse event management
- Nutrition: community nutrition assessment, deficiency prevention, therapeutic nutrition
- Maternal and child health: prenatal care protocols, well-child checks, growth monitoring
- Mental health: community mental health program, PTSD management, crisis intervention, grief support
- Health education: designing and delivering community health education programs
- Chronic disease management: hypertension, diabetes, asthma — long-term management protocols

#### Assessment
- Conduct an epidemiological investigation of a simulated outbreak
- Test and assess community water quality
- Design a community health education program
- Manage chronic disease patients using protocols

---

### Module 7: Clinical Rotation (Months 20-24)
**Duration**: 5 months (approximately 500 clinical hours)
**Materials**: Community medical facility, supervising practitioner, patient care protocols
**Survival Relevance**: The clinical rotation transforms knowledge into competence through supervised patient care

#### Structure
Under direct supervision of the community's medical practitioner(s):
- First 2 months: Observe and assist. Take histories, perform assessments, document encounters. All plans reviewed by supervisor before implementation.
- Months 3-4: Increasing independence. Manage routine patients with supervisor review. Handle after-hours calls with supervisor backup. Begin managing chronic disease patients.
- Month 5: Near-independent practice. Manage patient panel with supervisor oversight. Handle emergencies with supervisor available. Demonstrate readiness for independent practice under protocol.

#### Rotation Areas
- General clinic (acute illness, chronic disease, preventive care)
- Emergency/trauma response
- Obstetric and newborn care
- Mental health and counseling
- Pharmacy operations
- Public health and community outreach

#### Assessment
- 500 documented patient encounters with supervisor evaluation
- Supervisor assessment of clinical competence (monthly evaluations)
- Patient satisfaction feedback
- Case presentation of 5 complex patients
- Pass comprehensive clinical examination (written and practical)
- Demonstrated competence in emergency management
- Ready for independent practice under community medical protocols

---

## Graduation Requirements
- All 7 modules completed with demonstrated competence
- 500+ supervised clinical hours
- Comprehensive examination passed (written and practical)
- Community medical leadership certifies readiness
- Teaching requirement: deliver 10 medical education sessions to community members
- Medical ethics essay: 2,000 words on ethical decision-making in resource-limited settings
