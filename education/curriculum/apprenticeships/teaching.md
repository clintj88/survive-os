# Teaching Apprenticeship — 2-Year Intensive Program

## Overview
The teaching apprenticeship produces community educators capable of planning curriculum, managing classrooms, assessing student learning, and teaching across multiple subjects and grade levels. This is arguably the most important apprenticeship — without competent teachers, the entire education system fails within a generation.

**Duration**: 2 years (alongside Grade 11-12 coursework)
**Prerequisites**: Completion of advanced teaching-pedagogy Unit 1-3, demonstrated teaching aptitude, strong content knowledge in at least 2 subject areas
**Outcome**: Community teacher capable of independent classroom management and instruction

---

## Year 1: Foundations of Teaching Practice

### Module 1: Child and Adolescent Development (Months 1-3)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Developmental psychology references, observation access to all grade bands
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding how children develop enables appropriate instruction, prevents harm, and maximizes learning

#### Learning Objectives
- Describe cognitive, social, emotional, and physical development from ages 5-18
- Identify developmental milestones and recognize developmental delays
- Adapt teaching approaches for different developmental stages
- Support children who have experienced trauma

#### Content
- Cognitive development: concrete to abstract thinking, memory development, attention span by age, metacognition
- Social development: peer relationships, group dynamics, identity formation, moral reasoning
- Emotional development: emotional regulation, resilience, attachment, self-concept
- Physical development: growth patterns, motor skill development, nutrition and health effects on learning
- Trauma-informed teaching: recognizing trauma responses, creating safe spaces, avoiding re-traumatization, building trust
- Special needs: learning differences (dyslexia, dyscalculia, attention difficulties), gifted learners, English language learners, physical disabilities — strategies for each
- Cultural responsiveness: respecting diverse family backgrounds, incorporating community knowledge, avoiding bias

#### Assessment
- Complete developmental observation reports for 3 different age groups
- Design instruction adapted for 3 different developmental stages
- Create a trauma-responsive classroom plan
- Pass developmental psychology examination

---

### Module 2: Curriculum Design and Lesson Planning (Months 4-6)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Existing curriculum documents, lesson plan templates, assessment references
**Survival Relevance**: Good curriculum design ensures essential knowledge is transmitted efficiently and completely

#### Learning Objectives
- Design curriculum units using backward design principles
- Write measurable learning objectives at all levels of Bloom's taxonomy
- Create lesson plans with appropriate structure and differentiation
- Align assessment with learning objectives

#### Content
- Backward design: identify desired results → determine acceptable evidence → plan learning experiences
- Learning objectives: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, aligned to curriculum goals
- Bloom's taxonomy applied: remember, understand, apply, analyze, evaluate, create — designing activities at each level
- Lesson structure: engage, explore, explain, elaborate, evaluate (5E model)
- Unit design: scope, sequence, pacing, spiraling, integration across subjects
- Differentiation: tiered instruction, flexible grouping, learning stations, scaffolding, extension
- Materials development: creating handouts, visual aids, manipulatives, assessment tools from available resources
- Curriculum adaptation: modifying existing curriculum for different contexts, abilities, and resources

#### Assessment
- Design a complete 6-week unit using backward design
- Write lesson plans for 20 lessons with all components
- Create differentiated versions of 5 lessons
- Develop instructional materials from locally available resources

---

### Module 3: Classroom Management and Organization (Months 7-9)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Classroom access, management references, observation tools
**Survival Relevance**: In post-collapse education, classrooms are often multi-age, large, under-resourced, and filled with children who have experienced trauma. Management skills are essential.

#### Learning Objectives
- Establish productive learning environments in challenging conditions
- Manage multi-age classrooms effectively
- Prevent and address behavioral challenges constructively
- Organize instructional time for maximum learning

#### Content
- Physical environment: space arrangement for different activities, materials organization, display as learning tool
- Routines and procedures: entry, transitions, materials distribution, cleanup, dismissal — practiced until automatic
- Expectations: co-created rules, positive framing, consistent enforcement, logical consequences
- Multi-age management: station rotation, peer tutoring, independent work, whole-group activities that span ages
- Behavior prevention: engaging instruction, clear expectations, relationship building, proactive scanning
- Behavior intervention: proximity, redirect, private conversation, logical consequence, restorative conversation
- Crisis management: de-escalation, safety protocols, when to seek help
- Time management: bell-to-bell instruction, transition efficiency, flexible scheduling
- Record keeping: attendance, grades/mastery records, behavior logs, parent communication

#### Assessment
- Design a complete classroom management plan for a multi-age classroom
- Demonstrate management skills during 10 observed lessons
- Manage a classroom independently for 1 week
- Respond appropriately to 5 simulated behavior scenarios

---

### Module 4: Assessment and Data-Driven Instruction (Months 10-12)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Assessment design references, student work samples, data analysis tools
**Survival Relevance**: In a mastery-based system, assessment quality determines whether students actually learn life-critical skills

#### Learning Objectives
- Design valid assessments aligned to learning objectives
- Use formative assessment to guide daily instruction
- Analyze assessment data to identify learning gaps
- Implement mastery-based progression systems

#### Content
- Assessment types: formative (ongoing, guides instruction), summative (end of unit, measures mastery), diagnostic (beginning, identifies starting points), authentic (real-world performance tasks)
- Assessment design: alignment to objectives, validity (measures what it claims), reliability (consistent results), fairness (no bias)
- Rubric design: criteria, performance levels, descriptors — for written, oral, and practical assessments
- Formative assessment techniques: exit tickets, check-ins, observation checklists, peer assessment, self-assessment
- Data analysis: error pattern analysis, item analysis, class trend analysis, individual progress tracking
- Responsive teaching: using assessment data to adjust instruction, re-teach, extend, differentiate
- Mastery tracking: clear criteria, multiple attempts, specific feedback, progress documentation
- Reporting: communicating progress to students, families, and community leadership
- Portfolio assessment: design, curation, evaluation, student reflection

#### Assessment
- Design a complete assessment system for a unit (diagnostic, formative, summative, authentic)
- Create rubrics for 5 different assessment types
- Analyze a set of student work data and design responsive instruction
- Build a mastery-tracking system for a subject area

## Year 2: Teaching Practicum

### Module 5: Practicum Phase 1 — Assisted Teaching (Months 13-17)
**Duration**: 20 weeks
**Materials**: Assigned classroom placement, lesson planning materials, observation/reflection journals
**Survival Relevance**: Teaching skill develops through practice, feedback, and reflection — not through study alone

#### Structure
**Months 13-14: Observation and Assistance**
- Observe master teacher daily, documenting techniques and decisions
- Assist with small groups, individual students, materials preparation
- Learn classroom routines, student names, individual needs
- Write detailed observation reflections

**Months 15-17: Co-Teaching**
- Plan and deliver lesson segments under supervision
- Progress from structured segments (leading review, facilitating practice) to full lessons
- Receive detailed feedback after each teaching episode
- Begin managing classroom independently for short periods
- Minimum: 15 complete lessons delivered

#### Assessment
- Observation journal with analytical reflections
- 15+ lessons planned and delivered with feedback documentation
- Growth documented from first to last lesson
- Supervising teacher mid-term evaluation

---

### Module 6: Practicum Phase 2 — Lead Teaching (Months 18-22)
**Duration**: 20 weeks
**Materials**: Full classroom responsibility, curriculum resources, assessment tools
**Survival Relevance**: This phase proves the apprentice can teach independently — the community's future depends on it

#### Structure
**Months 18-20: Lead Teaching Under Observation**
- Take primary responsibility for instruction
- Plan and teach complete units (4+ weeks each)
- Manage classroom independently (supervisor observes but does not intervene except for safety)
- Assess student learning and adjust instruction based on results
- Minimum: 2 complete units designed and taught, 20+ lessons delivered

**Months 21-22: Independent Teaching**
- Full independent classroom responsibility for an extended period
- Supervisor available for consultation but not present
- Manage all aspects: planning, instruction, assessment, behavior, communication
- Demonstrate ability to handle unexpected situations independently

#### Assessment
- 2+ complete units designed and taught independently
- Student learning outcomes documented (pre/post assessment data)
- Classroom management demonstrated consistently over extended period
- Supervisor final evaluation of teaching readiness

---

### Module 7: Professional Development and Portfolio (Months 23-24)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Portfolio compilation materials, teaching philosophy references, community presentation materials
**Survival Relevance**: Reflective practice and professional growth ensure teaching quality improves over time

#### Content
- Teaching philosophy: articulate beliefs about learning, teaching, and community responsibility
- Professional reflection: systematic analysis of teaching practice, identification of strengths and growth areas
- Portfolio compilation: lesson plans, student work samples, assessment data, observation feedback, reflective essays, teaching philosophy
- Peer learning: observe and provide feedback to fellow apprentices
- Community presentation: present teaching philosophy and evidence of effectiveness
- Ongoing development plan: identify areas for continued growth, set professional goals
- Curriculum contribution: create or improve curriculum materials for community use

#### Requirements
- Teaching portfolio with:
  - Teaching philosophy statement (2,000 words)
  - 10 exemplary lesson plans with reflections
  - Student work samples showing learning growth
  - Assessment data demonstrating student outcomes
  - Observation feedback from supervising teacher
  - Reflective analysis of teaching growth
  - Curriculum materials contributed to community
- 35+ complete lessons delivered across the two-year practicum
- 2+ complete units designed, taught, and assessed independently
- Students taught demonstrate measurable learning growth
- Supervising teacher recommends candidate as ready for independent teaching

#### Assessment
- Portfolio demonstrates growth, competence, and reflective practice
- Presentation to assessment panel demonstrates teaching philosophy and evidence of effectiveness
- Supervising teacher final recommendation
- Student feedback (age-appropriate)
- Community education leadership certifies readiness
- Comprehensive teaching examination passed (written demonstration of pedagogical knowledge and practical teaching observation)

---

## Graduation Requirements
- All 7 modules completed with demonstrated competence
- 35+ lessons delivered with documented growth
- 2+ complete units independently designed and taught
- Comprehensive portfolio with all required components
- Teaching philosophy presented and defended
- Supervising teacher certifies readiness for independent teaching
- Community education leadership certifies readiness
- Ongoing professional development plan created
