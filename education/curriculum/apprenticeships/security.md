# Security Apprenticeship — 1-Year Intensive Program

## Overview
The security apprenticeship produces community security specialists capable of tactical planning, communication systems operation, perimeter security, conflict resolution, drone operation, and leadership under stress. Graduates serve as community security team leaders.

**Duration**: 1 year (alongside Grade 11 or 12 coursework)
**Prerequisites**: Completion of intermediate engineering-trades, communication-tech, and health-first-aid; demonstrated physical fitness and emotional maturity
**Outcome**: Community security team leader capable of independent operations

---

## Module 1: Tactical Planning and Situational Awareness (Months 1-2)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Maps, planning materials, communication equipment, observation tools
**Survival Relevance**: Security planning prevents threats from becoming crises

#### Learning Objectives
- Conduct threat assessments for the community
- Develop security plans for routine operations and contingencies
- Maintain situational awareness through systematic observation
- Brief and debrief security operations

#### Content
- Threat assessment: identify potential threats (natural disaster, wildlife, hostile groups, internal conflict), evaluate likelihood and impact, prioritize responses
- Security planning: perimeter analysis, entry/exit control, observation posts, patrol routes, communication plans
- Situational awareness: systematic observation, pattern recognition, anomaly detection, environmental cues
- Intelligence gathering: observation reports, pattern analysis, information sharing with neighboring communities
- Briefing format: situation, mission, execution, logistics, command/communication
- After-action review: what happened, what went well, what to improve, action items

#### Assessment
- Conduct a complete community threat assessment
- Develop a security plan with contingencies
- Maintain observation logs for 2 weeks with pattern analysis
- Brief and debrief 5 security operations

---

## Module 2: Communication Systems Operation (Months 3-4)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Radio equipment, Meshtastic devices, signal equipment, communication protocols
**Survival Relevance**: Communication is the nervous system of security — without it, response is uncoordinated

#### Learning Objectives
- Operate all community communication systems proficiently
- Manage communication during routine operations and emergencies
- Maintain and troubleshoot communication equipment
- Encrypt and secure communications when necessary

#### Content
- Radio operation: HF, VHF, UHF — proper technique, frequency management, scheduled contacts
- Meshtastic: device operation, network monitoring, message routing, node maintenance
- Signal systems: visual signals (flags, lights, mirrors), audio signals (horns, bells), code systems
- Communication protocols: routine, urgent, emergency — different procedures for each
- Communication security: code words, encryption basics, information compartmentalization
- Equipment maintenance: battery management, antenna care, radio troubleshooting
- Emergency communication: distress calls, mutual aid requests, coordination with neighboring communities
- Communication log maintenance: documenting all significant communications

#### Assessment
- Operate all community communication systems without assistance
- Manage emergency communication during a simulated crisis
- Troubleshoot and repair a communication equipment fault
- Maintain communication logs to standard

---

## Module 3: Physical Security and Emergency Response (Months 5-7)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Physical fitness equipment, first aid supplies, emergency response gear
**Survival Relevance**: Physical capability and emergency response skills are the foundation of community protection

#### Learning Objectives
- Maintain physical fitness standards for security operations
- Respond to fire, medical, weather, and security emergencies
- Conduct search and rescue operations
- Manage community evacuation

#### Content
- Physical fitness: endurance, strength, agility — maintained through daily training
- Fire response: fire behavior, suppression techniques, wildfire defense, structural fire response
- Medical first aid: trauma care review, triage, casualty evacuation, mass casualty management
- Weather emergencies: severe storm response, flooding response, extreme heat/cold response
- Security emergencies: perimeter breach response, hostile approach, internal disturbance
- Search and rescue: search patterns, navigation, casualty location, extraction, care in the field
- Evacuation: planning, routes, assembly points, accountability, vulnerable population management
- Night operations: navigation, observation, communication, safety
- De-escalation: verbal techniques, body language, conflict management before physical response

#### Assessment
- Meet physical fitness standards
- Lead response to 5 simulated emergency scenarios
- Complete a search and rescue exercise
- Plan and execute a community evacuation drill

---

## Module 4: Drone Operation and Reconnaissance (Months 7-8)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Drones (if available), mapping tools, observation equipment
**Survival Relevance**: Aerial observation extends the community's awareness far beyond what ground patrols can achieve

#### Learning Objectives
- Operate reconnaissance drones safely and effectively
- Plan and execute aerial observation missions
- Interpret aerial imagery for security and resource assessment
- Maintain drone equipment

#### Content
- Drone operation: pre-flight checks, launch, controlled flight, landing, emergency procedures
- Mission planning: objectives, routes, altitude, weather assessment, battery management
- Observation techniques: systematic area search, target identification, pattern recognition
- Image interpretation: terrain analysis, activity detection, change detection, resource mapping
- Equipment maintenance: battery care, propeller inspection, firmware management, repair
- Legal and ethical considerations: privacy, proportionality, rules of engagement for observation
- Alternative reconnaissance: ground observation, elevated observation posts, listening posts

#### Assessment
- Safely operate drones for reconnaissance missions
- Plan and execute observation missions with useful intelligence products
- Interpret aerial imagery and produce actionable reports
- Maintain drone equipment to operational standard

---

## Module 5: Conflict Resolution and Community Relations (Months 9-10)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Mediation training materials, roleplay scenarios, community relations frameworks
**Survival Relevance**: Most security incidents are internal disputes. Resolution skills prevent escalation.

#### Learning Objectives
- Mediate disputes using principled negotiation
- Manage internal community conflicts before they escalate
- Build positive relationships between security team and community
- Navigate ethical dilemmas in security operations

#### Content
- Conflict resolution: active listening, interest identification, option generation, agreement building
- Mediation: neutral third-party facilitation, structured mediation process, managing emotions
- De-escalation: recognizing escalation signals, verbal techniques, creating space, calling for support
- Community policing principles: security serves the community, relationships build trust, prevention over reaction
- Use of force: proportionality, necessity, last resort, documentation, accountability
- Ethics: balancing security and liberty, avoiding abuse of authority, transparency, accountability
- Cross-reference with advanced history.md governance content: historical examples of security forces that served communities vs those that oppressed them

#### Assessment
- Mediate 5 simulated disputes to resolution
- Demonstrate de-escalation techniques in realistic scenarios
- Pass ethics examination covering use of force, accountability, and community relations
- Develop a community relations plan for the security team

---

## Module 6: Leadership Under Stress (Months 11-12)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Scenario training materials, stress exercises, leadership evaluation tools
**Survival Relevance**: Security leaders must make good decisions under pressure — lives depend on it

#### Content
- Decision-making under stress: time-constrained decision frameworks, managing incomplete information
- Team leadership: task delegation, maintaining morale, managing fear, post-incident support
- Multi-agency coordination: working with medical, engineering, agricultural, and governance teams during crises
- Sustained operations: shift management, fatigue awareness, personnel rotation, self-care
- Training management: designing and delivering security training for community members
- Post-incident management: after-action reviews, psychological debriefing, lessons learned, procedure updates
- Capstone exercise: 48-hour security scenario incorporating all skills learned

#### Assessment
- Lead teams through 3 complex simulated scenarios
- Coordinate multi-team response to a community crisis simulation
- Complete 48-hour capstone exercise demonstrating sustained leadership
- Design and deliver a security training session for community members
- Pass comprehensive security examination (written and practical)

---

## Graduation Requirements
- All 6 modules completed with demonstrated competence
- Physical fitness standards maintained throughout program
- Comprehensive examination passed (written and practical)
- Community security leadership certifies readiness
- Teaching requirement: deliver 5 security awareness sessions to community
- Ethics essay: 1,500 words on the balance between security and community liberty
