# Engineering Apprenticeship — 2-Year Intensive Program

## Overview
The engineering apprenticeship produces community infrastructure specialists capable of designing, building, and maintaining critical systems: structures, water, power, mechanical, and communication equipment. Graduates can independently manage construction projects and maintain community infrastructure.

**Duration**: 2 years (alongside Grade 11-12 coursework)
**Prerequisites**: Completion of advanced engineering through Grade 10, demonstrated proficiency in technical drawing and mathematics through trigonometry
**Outcome**: Community infrastructure engineer capable of independent project management

---

## Year 1: Core Engineering Disciplines

### Module 1: Structural Engineering and Construction (Months 1-4)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Construction tools, building materials, survey equipment, engineering references
**Survival Relevance**: Safe structures protect lives. Structural failure kills.

#### Learning Objectives
- Design structures using engineering calculations (load analysis, beam sizing, foundation design)
- Lead construction projects from foundation through finish
- Inspect existing structures for safety and recommend repairs
- Read and create professional construction documents

#### Content
- Advanced load analysis: dead, live, wind, seismic, snow, combinations
- Foundation engineering: soil bearing capacity, footing design, pier design, drainage
- Framing systems: platform frame, post-and-beam, timber frame — design and construction
- Roof systems: truss design, rafter sizing, load paths, weatherproofing
- Concrete: mix design, formwork, reinforcement, placing, finishing, curing
- Masonry: brick, block, stone — structural design and construction technique
- Building envelope: insulation, moisture management, ventilation, weatherproofing
- Construction management: scheduling, material procurement, quality control, safety
- Structural inspection: assessment checklist, common failure modes, repair recommendations

#### Assessment
- Design a complete structure with engineering calculations
- Lead construction of a structural element for a community project
- Inspect 3 existing structures and produce assessment reports
- Pass structural engineering examination (written and practical)

---

### Module 2: Water and Plumbing Systems (Months 5-7)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Plumbing tools, pipe and fittings, pump equipment, water testing supplies
**Survival Relevance**: Clean water is the most critical infrastructure. Contaminated water kills faster than almost anything.

#### Learning Objectives
- Design complete water systems (source to waste treatment)
- Install and maintain plumbing systems
- Test water quality and manage treatment systems
- Design and build irrigation systems

#### Content
- Water source development: well drilling/digging principles, spring collection, surface water intake, rainwater harvesting
- Water treatment: filtration (sand, ceramic, activated carbon), disinfection (chlorination, UV, boiling), testing protocols
- Distribution: pipe sizing (flow calculations), pressure management, pump selection and installation, gravity-fed systems
- Plumbing: building water supply (hot and cold), fixture installation, drainage and venting, backflow prevention
- Waste treatment: septic systems, composting toilets, greywater recycling, constructed wetlands
- Irrigation: drip, sprinkler, gravity-fed — design, installation, and maintenance
- Hydraulic calculations: flow rate, pressure, friction loss, pump head

#### Assessment
- Design a complete water system for a community building
- Install plumbing in a building to functional standard
- Test water quality using available methods
- Design and install an irrigation system
- Pass water systems examination

---

### Module 3: Electrical and Power Systems (Months 8-10)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Electrical tools, wire and components, solar panels, batteries, inverters, generators
**Survival Relevance**: Electrical power enables medical equipment, communication, lighting, and food preservation

#### Learning Objectives
- Design and install building electrical systems safely
- Design and install off-grid power systems (solar, wind, micro-hydro)
- Maintain generators and power equipment
- Troubleshoot electrical faults systematically

#### Content
- Electrical theory review: voltage, current, resistance, power, AC/DC, single/three phase
- Building wiring: service entrance, distribution panels, branch circuits, wire sizing, overcurrent protection, grounding, code compliance (NEC principles)
- Solar power: panel sizing, charge controller selection, battery bank design, inverter selection, system commissioning
- Wind power: turbine selection, tower design, controller configuration, grid-tie vs battery
- Micro-hydro: head and flow measurement, turbine selection, penstock design, power output calculation
- Generator: operation, maintenance, fuel management, load management
- Battery systems: lead-acid, lithium — charging profiles, maintenance, lifecycle management
- Troubleshooting: systematic fault-finding, safety procedures, test equipment use

#### Assessment
- Design and wire a building electrical system
- Design and install a solar power system with battery storage
- Maintain and troubleshoot generators
- Pass electrical safety and competency examination

---

### Module 4: Metalworking and Fabrication (Months 11-12)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Forge, anvil, metalworking tools, welding equipment (if available), raw metal stock
**Survival Relevance**: The ability to shape metal is fundamental to maintaining and creating tools, equipment, and infrastructure

#### Learning Objectives
- Forge functional tools from raw stock
- Weld, braze, and solder for structural and repair applications
- Fabricate metal components for community infrastructure
- Heat-treat metals for optimal properties

#### Content
- Forge work: drawing out, upsetting, bending, punching, splitting, forge welding
- Tool making: axes, chisels, hammers, agricultural tools, hardware
- Welding: arc welding (if equipment available), oxy-fuel welding and cutting
- Brazing and soldering: techniques, joint design, applications
- Sheet metal: layout, cutting, bending, joining, ductwork and flashing
- Heat treatment: annealing, hardening, tempering — achieving desired properties
- Metal casting: sand casting basics, pattern making, pouring technique

#### Assessment
- Forge 5 functional tools to community-use standard
- Demonstrate welding competency (if equipment available)
- Fabricate a sheet metal component for a building project
- Heat-treat a tool to achieve specified hardness

## Year 2: Advanced Systems and Capstone

### Module 5: Mechanical Systems (Months 13-16)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Engines, pumps, mills, vehicle access, workshop tools
**Survival Relevance**: Mechanical systems multiply community capability — water pumping, grain milling, wood processing, transportation

#### Content
- Engine theory and overhaul: internal combustion (gasoline and diesel), operation, maintenance, rebuild
- Power transmission: belts, chains, gears, shafts, bearings, clutches — design and maintenance
- Pumps: centrifugal, piston, diaphragm, ram — selection, installation, maintenance, repair
- Mills: grain mill, saw mill — design, construction, power connection
- Vehicle maintenance: systematic diagnosis, preventive maintenance, common repairs
- Hydraulic systems: principles, components, design, maintenance
- Alternative power: water wheel, wind turbine mechanical drive, animal power coupling

#### Assessment
- Rebuild a small engine to operational standard
- Design and build a power transmission system
- Install and commission a pump system
- Perform vehicle maintenance and repairs

---

### Module 6: Communication Equipment (Months 17-19)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Radio equipment, electronic components, test equipment, antenna materials
**Survival Relevance**: Communication equipment maintenance keeps the community connected

#### Content
- Radio transceiver maintenance: alignment, component replacement, troubleshooting
- Antenna construction: dipole, Yagi, vertical — build, test, install
- Power supply design and repair: regulated supplies, battery charging systems
- Electronic troubleshooting: systematic fault-finding, component testing, soldering skills
- Meshtastic infrastructure: node construction, solar power system, weatherproof enclosure, deployment
- Network equipment: switches, routers, cabling — installation and maintenance

#### Assessment
- Repair a radio transceiver to operational standard
- Build and install an antenna system with measured performance
- Build and deploy a solar-powered Meshtastic node
- Troubleshoot electronic equipment systematically

---

### Module 7: Capstone Infrastructure Project (Months 20-24)
**Duration**: 5 months
**Materials**: All available engineering resources
**Survival Relevance**: The capstone produces real community infrastructure

#### Structure
Each apprentice designs and builds a significant infrastructure project for the community:
- **Building**: Complete structure from foundation through finish
- **Water system**: Well, pump house, treatment system, or irrigation
- **Power system**: Solar array, wind installation, or micro-hydro
- **Mechanical system**: Mill, forge installation, or workshop
- **Communication**: Radio station, antenna farm, or network infrastructure

#### Requirements
- Complete engineering design with calculations
- Professional drawings (plan, elevation, section, detail)
- Materials procurement and scheduling
- Construction/installation with quality control
- Testing, commissioning, and performance verification
- Operations and maintenance manual
- As-built documentation
- Community handover with operator training
- Teaching: deliver 5 engineering lessons to younger students
- Mentor at least 2 intermediate band students in trade skills

#### Assessment
- Project completed to engineering specification
- Documentation package complete and professional
- Community accepts the project and operators are trained
- Teaching delivered to younger students
- Comprehensive engineering examination passed (written and practical)
