# Advanced Band (Grades 9-12, Ages 14-18) — Economics

## Overview
Advanced economics prepares students for community economic management. The curriculum progresses from microeconomic theory through macroeconomic planning to a capstone economic plan for a community of 500 people. All content cross-references the History curriculum's economic analysis while focusing on practical applied skills.

## Grade 9: Microeconomics

### Unit 1: Supply, Demand, and Markets (Weeks 1-18)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Simulation supplies, graph paper, calculator, community economic data
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding markets enables fair trade, efficient resource allocation, and detection of economic manipulation

#### Learning Objectives
- Analyze supply and demand with mathematical precision (elasticity, equilibrium calculation)
- Understand market structures (perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly) and their effects
- Apply consumer and producer surplus concepts
- Evaluate market failures and potential government/community interventions

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Advanced Supply and Demand**
- Activity: Price elasticity of demand (how much does quantity demanded change when price changes?). Elastic (luxury goods — demand changes a lot) vs inelastic (necessities — demand barely changes). Cross-price elasticity (how does the price of one good affect demand for another?).
- Practice: Calculate elasticities for community goods and interpret implications

**Lesson 5-8: Market Structures**
- Activity: Perfect competition (many sellers, identical goods, no price control). Monopoly (one seller, controls price). Oligopoly (few sellers, interdependent). How structure affects price, quality, and access. Community examples of each.
- Practice: Analyze community markets and identify structures

**Lesson 9-12: Surplus and Welfare**
- Activity: Consumer surplus (benefit to buyers above what they pay), producer surplus (benefit to sellers above their costs). Total welfare maximized at equilibrium. Deadweight loss from price controls, taxes, or monopoly.
- Practice: Calculate surplus and analyze welfare effects of community policies

**Lesson 13-18: Market Failures and Interventions**
- Activity: Externalities (pollution, education benefits), public goods (defense, sanitation), common pool resources (fisheries, grazing land). When markets fail, what interventions work? Price controls, quotas, taxes/subsidies, regulation, commons governance.
- Practice: Identify market failures in community context and propose evidence-based interventions

#### Assessment
- Calculate elasticity and interpret implications
- Analyze market structures and their effects
- Evaluate market failures and propose interventions

## Grade 10: Macroeconomics

### Unit 2: Community-Scale Resource Management (Weeks 1-18)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Community economic data, planning templates, calculator
**Survival Relevance**: Macroeconomic management at the community scale determines whether resources are allocated efficiently, sustainably, and fairly

#### Learning Objectives
- Measure community economic output (total production, per capita, by sector)
- Manage labor allocation across sectors (agriculture, construction, security, education, medical)
- Plan long-term resource investment and capital formation
- Analyze trade balances and terms of trade with other communities

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Measuring Community Economy**
- Activity: GDP equivalent for community: total value of goods and services produced. Track by sector. Per capita analysis. Trend analysis — is production growing or declining?
- Practice: Calculate community economic output for the current period

**Lesson 5-8: Labor Allocation**
- Activity: How many people work in each sector? Is the allocation optimal? What happens if we shift 10% of labor from agriculture to construction? Opportunity cost analysis. Seasonal labor variation planning.
- Practice: Analyze current labor allocation and propose optimizations

**Lesson 9-12: Capital Formation**
- Activity: Capital = durable productive assets (tools, buildings, infrastructure, livestock). Investment in capital increases future production but costs current resources. How much should a community invest vs consume? Depreciation and replacement planning.
- Practice: Create a capital investment plan with depreciation schedules

**Lesson 13-18: Trade Analysis**
- Activity: Terms of trade (what you give vs what you get). Trade balance (value of exports vs imports). Trade dependency analysis (what happens if a trading partner disappears?). Diversification strategy.
- Practice: Analyze community trade relationships and propose improvements

#### Assessment
- Measure and analyze community economic output
- Optimize labor allocation with opportunity cost analysis
- Create capital investment and trade strategy plans

## Grade 11: Accounting and Policy

### Unit 3: Accounting and Financial Management (Weeks 1-18)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Ledgers, financial statements templates, community financial records
**Survival Relevance**: Accurate accounting prevents resource waste, enables informed decisions, and builds community trust through transparency

#### Learning Objectives
- Maintain double-entry accounting systems
- Prepare financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow)
- Perform financial analysis (ratios, trends, forecasting)
- Design financial reporting systems for community transparency

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-6: Double-Entry Accounting**
- Activity: Every transaction affects two accounts. Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Debits on the left, credits on the right. Journal entries, posting to ledger, trial balance.
- Practice: Maintain a complete accounting system for a community operation for 1 month

**Lesson 7-12: Financial Statements**
- Activity: Balance sheet (what we own vs what we owe at a point in time). Income statement (what we produced minus what we consumed over a period). Cash flow statement (where resources came from and where they went).
- Practice: Prepare all three financial statements from accounting records

**Lesson 13-18: Financial Analysis and Reporting**
- Activity: Financial ratios (liquidity, efficiency, sustainability). Trend analysis (are we getting stronger or weaker?). Forecasting (projecting future financial position). Public financial reporting for community transparency.
- Practice: Analyze community finances and present findings to leadership

#### Assessment
- Maintain accurate double-entry accounting
- Prepare financial statements from records
- Perform financial analysis and present findings

### Unit 4: Trade Policy and Currency Systems (Weeks 19-34)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Negotiation materials, policy drafting supplies, currency design materials
**Survival Relevance**: Trade policy and currency systems structure inter-community economic relationships and determine whether exchange is fair and sustainable

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-6: Trade Policy**
- Activity: Free trade vs protectionism (trade-offs of each). Trade agreements (bilateral, multilateral). Tariffs and their effects. Strategic trade considerations (don't become dependent on imports of critical goods). Sanctions and economic leverage.
- Practice: Draft trade policy recommendations for community leadership

**Lesson 7-12: Currency Systems**
- Activity: Design a currency system: commodity-backed (redeemable for stored goods), fiat (declared valuable by authority), time-based (labor hours), hybrid. Address: issuance, inflation control, counterfeiting prevention, conversion to other communities' currencies.
- Practice: Design a complete currency system with economic justification

**Lesson 13-16: International Economics**
- Activity: Apply international economic concepts to inter-community relations: comparative advantage (each community should produce what they're relatively best at), gains from trade, trade creation and diversion, economic unions.
- Practice: Analyze inter-community trade using comparative advantage theory

#### Assessment
- Draft trade policy with economic justification
- Design a currency system addressing all critical factors
- Apply comparative advantage to inter-community trade analysis

## Grade 12: Economic Capstone

### Unit 5: Capstone — Economic Plan for a Community of 500 (Full Year)
**Duration**: Full academic year
**Materials**: All available economic data and planning tools
**Survival Relevance**: This capstone produces a real, actionable economic plan for a large community

#### Structure
Develop a comprehensive economic plan covering:

1. **Economic Assessment**: Current production, consumption, trade, capital stock, labor force, natural resources
2. **Sector Plans**: Agriculture, manufacturing/trades, services, administration — production targets and resource requirements for each
3. **Labor Allocation**: How many people in each sector, seasonal variation, training pipeline
4. **Capital Investment Plan**: What to build/acquire, in what order, with what resources
5. **Trade Strategy**: What to export, what to import, with whom, on what terms
6. **Currency and Financial System**: Medium of exchange, accounting standards, financial reporting
7. **Social Safety Net**: Support for elderly, disabled, children, sick — how funded
8. **Risk Management**: Contingency plans for crop failure, trade disruption, population change, conflict
9. **Growth Projections**: 1-year, 5-year, 20-year economic outlook with scenarios
10. **Implementation Plan**: Who does what, when, with what authority and accountability

#### Requirements
- Data-driven analysis (not speculation)
- Historical evidence for recommendations (cross-reference with History curriculum)
- Stakeholder input (interview community members representing different sectors)
- Financial projections with sensitivity analysis
- Presented to community leadership for review and feedback
- Archived as a planning reference document

#### Assessment
- Comprehensive economic plan covering all 10 components
- Data-driven analysis with historical evidence
- Clear, actionable implementation plan
- Successful presentation to community leadership
- Peer evaluation of plan quality
