# Advanced Band (Grades 9-12, Ages 14-18) — Advanced Medicine

## Overview
This is the pre-medical track preparing students for the medical apprenticeship. Students progress from anatomy survey through pathophysiology and clinical skills to supervised patient care. By graduation, students should function at a community health worker level with preparation for advanced medical training.

## Grade 9: Foundations

### Unit 1: Anatomy and Physiology Survey (Weeks 1-14)
**Duration**: 14 weeks
**Materials**: Anatomical diagrams/models, clinical reference materials
**Survival Relevance**: Comprehensive anatomical knowledge is the foundation of all medical assessment, diagnosis, and treatment

#### Learning Objectives
- Describe all major body systems in detail (structure and function)
- Use correct medical terminology (anatomical position, directional terms, body regions)
- Correlate normal anatomy with common pathology
- Perform a systematic physical assessment

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Medical Terminology**
- Activity: Root words, prefixes, suffixes: "cardio" (heart), "pulmo" (lung), "-itis" (inflammation), "hyper-" (excess), "hypo-" (deficient). Anatomical position and directional terms (anterior/posterior, medial/lateral, superior/inferior, proximal/distal).
- Practice: Master 100 medical terms and use them correctly

**Lesson 3-14: Systems Review** (1-2 weeks per system)
- Cardiovascular, respiratory, neurological, musculoskeletal, gastrointestinal, renal, endocrine, reproductive, integumentary (skin), lymphatic/immune
- For each: detailed anatomy, normal physiology, common pathology, assessment techniques, survival-relevant conditions
- Practice: System-by-system assessments on classmates

#### Assessment
- Use medical terminology correctly
- Describe anatomy and physiology of all systems
- Perform a systematic head-to-toe physical assessment

### Unit 2: Patient Assessment and Vital Signs (Weeks 15-22)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, thermometer, watch with seconds, penlight
**Survival Relevance**: Accurate patient assessment is the single most important medical skill — correct treatment depends on correct assessment

#### Learning Objectives
- Obtain and interpret vital signs (pulse, blood pressure, respiratory rate, temperature, oxygen saturation if pulse oximeter available)
- Conduct a focused patient interview (SAMPLE history: Signs/Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past medical history, Last intake, Events leading to illness)
- Perform a head-to-toe physical examination
- Document findings using SOAP format (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Vital Signs**
- Activity: Measure pulse (rate, rhythm, quality), blood pressure (systolic/diastolic, normal ranges by age), respiratory rate (count for 30 seconds, multiply by 2), temperature (oral, axillary, rectal — advantages of each). Interpret: what do abnormal values indicate?
- Practice: Measure vital signs on 10 different people, interpret results

**Lesson 3-4: Patient Interview**
- Activity: SAMPLE history format. OPQRST for pain assessment (Onset, Provokes/Palliates, Quality, Region/Radiation, Severity, Time). Practice asking questions systematically without leading the patient.
- Practice: Conduct and document 5 patient interviews

**Lesson 5-6: Physical Examination**
- Activity: Head-to-toe systematic exam: head/eyes/ears/nose/throat → neck → chest (heart and lungs) → abdomen → extremities → neurological (mental status, cranial nerves, motor/sensory, reflexes).
- Practice: Perform complete physical exams on 5 classmates with documentation

**Lesson 7-8: Documentation**
- Activity: SOAP notes: Subjective (what the patient tells you), Objective (what you observe and measure), Assessment (what you think is wrong), Plan (what you're going to do). Clear, concise, accurate.
- Practice: Write SOAP notes for 10 patient encounters

#### Assessment
- Measure and interpret vital signs accurately
- Conduct a focused patient interview
- Perform a complete physical examination
- Document findings in SOAP format

## Grade 10: Pathophysiology and Pharmacology

### Unit 3: Pathophysiology (Weeks 1-18)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Clinical reference materials, case studies
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding how diseases affect the body enables recognition, prioritization, and appropriate treatment

#### Learning Objectives
- Explain the pathophysiology of common conditions in each body system
- Recognize clinical presentations (what the disease looks like)
- Differentiate between conditions with similar presentations
- Prioritize treatment based on urgency

#### Lessons (condensed — 2-3 weeks per system)
**Cardiovascular**: Heart failure, hypertension, shock (hypovolemic, cardiogenic, septic, anaphylactic), cardiac arrest, chest pain differential
**Respiratory**: Pneumonia, asthma/COPD, respiratory failure, pneumothorax, pulmonary embolism
**Neurological**: Stroke, seizures, head injury, meningitis, altered mental status differential
**Gastrointestinal**: Appendicitis, bowel obstruction, GI bleeding, dehydration, hepatitis
**Infectious Disease**: Common bacterial, viral, fungal infections — recognition, treatment, isolation
**Trauma**: Hemorrhagic shock, fracture complications (compartment syndrome, fat embolism), head injury, spinal injury
**Metabolic/Endocrine**: Diabetes (hypo/hyperglycemia), thyroid emergencies, electrolyte imbalances, malnutrition

For each: mechanism (what goes wrong at the cellular/organ level), clinical presentation (what you see/measure), differential diagnosis (what else could it be), treatment priorities, complications to watch for.

#### Assessment
- Explain pathophysiology for 20+ conditions
- Recognize clinical presentations and perform differential diagnosis
- Prioritize treatment based on urgency

### Unit 4: Pharmacology (Weeks 19-30)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Drug references, medication samples (expired acceptable for study), dosing calculators
**Survival Relevance**: Correct medication use saves lives; incorrect use kills. Understanding pharmacology enables safe prescribing with available medications.

#### Learning Objectives
- Explain drug classes and mechanisms of action
- Calculate correct drug dosing (weight-based, age-adjusted)
- Identify drug interactions and contraindications
- Manage a community pharmacy (inventory, storage, dispensing)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Drug Classes**
- Activity: Analgesics (NSAIDs, opioids), antibiotics (penicillins, macrolides, fluoroquinolones, tetracyclines), cardiovascular drugs, respiratory drugs, GI drugs, psychiatric medications, hormones, anesthetics. For each: mechanism, indications, side effects, contraindications.
- Practice: Create a drug reference card for each class with clinical decision points

**Lesson 5-8: Dosing and Administration**
- Activity: Weight-based dosing (mg/kg), age adjustments, route of administration (oral, IM, IV, topical). "A 70 kg adult gets amoxicillin 500mg TID. A 20 kg child gets amoxicillin 25mg/kg/day divided TID = 500mg/day = 167mg TID."
- Practice: Calculate doses for 20 scenarios with different patient weights and ages

**Lesson 9-12: Pharmacy Management**
- Activity: Inventory management (first-in-first-out, expiration tracking), proper storage (temperature, light protection, moisture), dispensing protocols (double-check, label clearly, patient education), record keeping.
- Practice: Manage a simulated pharmacy for 2 weeks with complete records

#### Assessment
- Explain mechanisms and indications for major drug classes
- Calculate correct doses for diverse patient populations
- Manage pharmacy operations with proper protocols

### Unit 5: Advanced First Aid and Trauma Care (Weeks 31-40)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Advanced first aid equipment, simulation supplies, manikins
**Survival Relevance**: Trauma is the leading cause of death in young adults in any community. Advanced trauma care saves the most lives with the greatest time-sensitivity.

#### Learning Objectives
- Perform advanced airway management (jaw thrust, nasopharyngeal airway, bag-valve-mask)
- Manage major trauma (hemorrhage control, chest injuries, abdominal injuries)
- Perform advanced splinting and fracture management
- Manage medical emergencies (allergic reactions, poisoning, environmental emergencies)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-3: Advanced Airway**
- Activity: Jaw thrust (for suspected spinal injury — opens airway without moving neck), nasopharyngeal airway (tube through nose to maintain airway), bag-valve-mask ventilation (manual breathing for the patient).
- Practice: Demonstrate all techniques on manikins

**Lesson 4-6: Major Trauma Management**
- Activity: Chest injuries (tension pneumothorax — decompress with needle, open chest wound — seal 3 sides), abdominal injuries (don't push organs back in, cover with moist dressing), pelvic fractures (immobilize, massive transfusion protocol if available).
- Practice: Manage simulated major trauma scenarios

**Lesson 7-8: Advanced Fracture Management**
- Activity: Traction splinting (femur fractures), joint reduction awareness (dislocations — recognize but refer to trained provider unless life/limb threatening), compartment syndrome recognition (hard swollen extremity, pain with passive stretch = emergency).
- Practice: Apply traction splints and recognize complications

**Lesson 9-10: Medical Emergencies**
- Activity: Anaphylaxis (epinephrine administration), diabetic emergencies (glucose for hypoglycemia), overdose/poisoning (supportive care, antidotes if available), seizure management, severe allergic reactions.
- Practice: Manage 5 medical emergency scenarios with complete patient care

#### Assessment
- Demonstrate advanced airway management techniques
- Manage major trauma scenarios with correct interventions
- Recognize and manage medical emergencies

## Grade 11: Clinical Skills

### Unit 6: Procedural Skills (Weeks 1-20)
**Duration**: 20 weeks
**Materials**: Suture practice kits, splinting materials, IV training arms (if available), obstetric simulation equipment, dental instruments
**Survival Relevance**: These procedural skills enable definitive care when no higher level of medical care is available

#### Learning Objectives
- Perform wound closure (suturing, stapling alternatives, butterfly closures)
- Apply advanced splinting and casting concepts
- Understand IV access theory and fluid management
- Recognize obstetric emergencies and provide initial management
- Perform basic dental procedures (extraction of loose teeth, abscess drainage)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-6: Wound Management**
- Activity: Wound assessment (can it be closed? Contamination level? Time since injury? Tension?). Closure techniques: simple interrupted sutures, horizontal mattress (for tension), butterfly strips (for minor wounds), staples (if available). Wound irrigation, debridement, anesthesia (local — lidocaine if available).
- Practice: Suture on practice pads until proficient (minimum 50 practice closures)

**Lesson 7-10: Splinting and Immobilization**
- Activity: Advanced splinting: sugar tong splint (forearm), thumb spica, knee immobilizer, figure-eight (clavicle). Plaster casting concept (if materials available). Sling and swathe variations.
- Practice: Apply all splint types correctly

**Lesson 11-14: IV Theory and Fluids**
- Activity: IV access theory: site selection, insertion technique (practice on training arms if available), securing, monitoring, complications (infiltration, phlebitis, infection). Fluid management: isotonic fluids for volume replacement, maintenance fluid calculations.
- Practice: Practice IV insertion technique, calculate fluid requirements

**Lesson 15-18: Obstetric Care**
- Activity: Normal labor management: stages of labor, monitoring, support. Emergency complications: postpartum hemorrhage (uterine massage, pressure, medications if available), cord prolapse, shoulder dystocia, breech delivery awareness. Newborn resuscitation basics (dry, stimulate, warm, ventilate if needed).
- Practice: Walk through delivery management and emergency scenarios

**Lesson 19-20: Dental Procedures**
- Activity: Tooth extraction (loose/damaged teeth only — proper technique with forceps), abscess incision and drainage, temporary fillings, dental pain management. Know limitations — complex procedures require trained dentists.
- Practice: Practice extraction technique on models

#### Assessment
- Suture wounds with correct technique
- Apply advanced splints and immobilization
- Demonstrate IV theory knowledge and fluid calculations
- Manage simulated obstetric scenarios
- Perform basic dental procedures on models

### Unit 7: Public Health, Epidemiology, and Mental Health (Weeks 21-34)
**Duration**: 14 weeks
**Materials**: Community health data, mental health assessment tools, case studies
**Survival Relevance**: Community health management prevents disease outbreaks. Mental health support maintains functional community members.

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Public Health and Epidemiology**
- Activity: Disease surveillance systems, outbreak investigation, quarantine and isolation protocols, vaccination principles (even if vaccines unavailable — understand the concept), sanitation inspection, water quality management.
- Practice: Design and implement a community disease surveillance system

**Lesson 5-8: Mental Health Assessment and Support**
- Activity: Psychiatric assessment (mental status exam), crisis intervention, suicide risk assessment (ask directly — it doesn't cause suicide), grief counseling, PTSD recognition and support, substance abuse assessment.
- Practice: Conduct mental health assessments, practice crisis intervention scenarios

**Lesson 9-14: Community Health Program**
- Activity: Design a comprehensive community health program: primary care (routine health maintenance), maternal and child health, chronic disease management, mental health services, dental care, health education.
- Practice: Present community health program to medical leadership

#### Assessment
- Design a disease surveillance system
- Conduct mental health assessments safely
- Present a comprehensive community health program

## Grade 12: Clinical Practicum

### Unit 8: Supervised Patient Care (Full Year)
**Duration**: Full academic year
**Materials**: Community medical facility access, clinical supplies, supervision by community medical provider
**Survival Relevance**: Clinical experience under supervision is the bridge from knowledge to competence

#### Structure
- Minimum 500 hours of supervised clinical time
- Rotate through: acute care (injuries, illness), chronic care (ongoing conditions), maternal health, pediatrics, mental health, dental, pharmacy
- Maintain patient logs documenting all encounters
- Weekly case presentations to supervisor and class
- Monthly competency assessments

#### Competencies Required for Completion
- Independent patient assessment (history, physical, vitals, documentation)
- Common wound management (cleaning, closure, dressing)
- Fracture and dislocation management (splinting, pain control, referral decisions)
- Medical emergency response (shock, respiratory distress, altered mental status, allergic reaction)
- Medication administration (correct drug, dose, route, documentation)
- Obstetric support (normal delivery assistance, emergency recognition)
- Mental health crisis response
- Patient education (explaining conditions and treatments to patients)
- Medical record keeping (complete, accurate, timely documentation)

### Unit 9: Teaching and Medical Ethics (Concurrent)
**Duration**: Ongoing throughout Grade 12
**Materials**: Teaching materials, ethics case studies

#### Teaching Practicum
- Plan and deliver first aid training to Building and Intermediate band students
- Create educational materials for community health education
- Mentor younger students in the medical track

#### Medical Ethics
- Case-based ethics discussions: confidentiality, informed consent, resource allocation, end-of-life decisions, triage decisions, scope of practice
- "When should you attempt treatment vs refer? When must you act despite uncertainty? When is it ethical to say 'I don't know'?"
- Professional boundaries, self-care, and managing the emotional burden of medical practice

#### Assessment
- Complete 500 supervised clinical hours
- Pass competency assessments in all required areas
- Deliver first aid training to younger students
- Participate in ethics discussions demonstrating reasoned, empathetic decision-making
- Supervisor evaluation of professional behavior, clinical judgment, and patient rapport
