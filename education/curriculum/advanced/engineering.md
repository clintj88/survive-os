# Advanced Band (Grades 9-12, Ages 14-18) — Engineering

## Overview
Advanced engineering prepares students to design, build, and maintain community infrastructure. The curriculum progresses from drafting and materials science through structural, mechanical, and electrical engineering to a capstone infrastructure project.

## Grade 9: Foundations

### Unit 1: Technical Drawing and Design (Weeks 1-12)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Drawing tools (T-square, triangles, compass, protractor, scale ruler), paper, pencils
**Survival Relevance**: Technical drawings are the universal language of building — without them, construction is guesswork

#### Learning Objectives
- Create professional-quality orthographic, isometric, and sectional drawings
- Dimension drawings following drafting conventions
- Design projects from concept through detailed drawings
- Create materials lists and construction schedules from drawings

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Drafting Fundamentals**
- Activity: Line types (object, hidden, center, dimension, section), lettering standards, title blocks, scale selection. Create orthographic projections with all required views.
- Practice: Create 10 complete drawing sets for simple objects

**Lesson 5-8: Advanced Drawing**
- Activity: Section views (cut through an object to show interior), detail views (enlarged view of complex areas), assembly drawings (showing how parts fit together), exploded views.
- Practice: Create section, detail, and assembly drawings for 5 projects

**Lesson 9-12: Design Process**
- Activity: Concept sketch → preliminary design → detailed drawings → materials list → construction schedule. Design a small community project through all phases.
- Practice: Complete a full design package for a community project

#### Assessment
- Create professional-quality technical drawings
- Design a project from concept through detailed drawings
- Produce a materials list and construction schedule

### Unit 2: Materials Science and Hand Tool Mastery (Weeks 13-28)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Various materials (wood, metal, stone, concrete), hand tools, workshop
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding material properties prevents structural failures and enables selection of the right material for each application

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify and describe properties of construction materials (wood, steel, concrete, stone, rope, leather)
- Select appropriate materials for specific applications
- Demonstrate mastery of hand tools in wood, metal, and stone working
- Perform basic welding, brazing, or soldering

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Material Properties**
- Activity: Test materials: tensile strength (pull apart), compressive strength (crush), hardness (scratch test), flexibility/brittleness, weather resistance, workability. Compare: wood (strong for weight, burns), steel (very strong, rusts), concrete (excellent compression, poor tension), stone (excellent compression, difficult to shape).
- Practice: Test and compare materials, create a reference chart

**Lesson 5-8: Wood and Metal Working**
- Activity: Advanced wood joinery (dovetails, finger joints, through-tenon). Precision metal work (filing to tolerance, drilling, tapping threads). Tool making (forge a chisel, file it to shape, heat treat).
- Practice: Create projects demonstrating advanced joinery and metalwork

**Lesson 9-12: Joining Technologies**
- Activity: Welding (if equipment available — arc or gas), brazing (lower temperature metal joining), soldering (electrical connections and sheet metal). Mechanical fasteners (bolts, rivets, nails, screws — when to use each).
- Practice: Create strong joints using each available method

**Lesson 13-16: Applied Materials**
- Activity: Concrete mixing and pouring (ratio, water content, curing). Rope work (splicing, knot strength, load capacity). Leather working (cutting, stitching, forming). Each produces community-useful items.
- Practice: Complete a project using concrete, rope, or leather

#### Assessment
- Identify material properties and select appropriate materials
- Demonstrate hand tool mastery in wood and metal
- Create strong joints using available joining methods

## Grade 10: Structural, Plumbing, and Electrical

### Unit 3: Structural Engineering (Weeks 1-16)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Construction tools, structural analysis materials, building site access
**Survival Relevance**: Structural engineering ensures buildings don't collapse — bad structures kill people

#### Learning Objectives
- Analyze structural loads (dead, live, wind, seismic, snow)
- Design foundations, walls, roofs, and floor systems
- Calculate beam sizing and column capacity
- Build structural elements to specification

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Load Analysis**
- Activity: Dead load (weight of the structure itself), live load (people, furniture, equipment), environmental loads (wind, snow, earthquake). Calculate total loads on a community building. Safety factor: design for 2-3× expected loads.
- Practice: Calculate loads for 5 community structures

**Lesson 5-8: Foundation and Wall Design**
- Activity: Foundation types: spread footing, pier, slab. Wall types: load-bearing vs non-load-bearing, stud wall, post-and-beam, masonry. Design foundations and walls for calculated loads.
- Practice: Design foundations and walls for a community building

**Lesson 9-12: Roof and Floor Systems**
- Activity: Roof types: gable, hip, shed, flat. Rafter sizing based on span and load. Truss design (triangulated structures). Floor systems: joist sizing, span tables, deflection limits.
- Practice: Design roof and floor systems with calculations

**Lesson 13-16: Construction Practice**
- Activity: Build structural elements for a community project — frame a wall, set rafters, pour a foundation pad. Apply engineering calculations to real construction.
- Practice: Participate in building structural elements with engineering oversight

#### Assessment
- Calculate structural loads for buildings
- Design structural systems (foundation, wall, roof, floor)
- Build structural elements to engineering specifications

### Unit 4: Plumbing, Electrical, and Power Systems (Weeks 17-32)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Plumbing supplies, electrical supplies, power system components
**Survival Relevance**: Water and power systems are critical infrastructure — designing and maintaining them is an essential engineering function

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-4: Plumbing Systems**
- Activity: Complete water system design: source → treatment → storage → distribution → drainage → waste treatment. Pipe sizing (flow calculations), pump selection, pressure management, backflow prevention.
- Practice: Design a complete plumbing system for a community building

**Lesson 5-8: Electrical Systems**
- Activity: Building wiring: service entrance, distribution panel, branch circuits, outlets, switches, lighting. Wire sizing (ampacity), overcurrent protection, grounding, code compliance (NEC principles even post-collapse — they're safety standards).
- Practice: Design and wire electrical circuits for a community building

**Lesson 9-12: Power Systems**
- Activity: Power generation: solar (panel sizing, inverter selection, battery bank design), wind (turbine selection, tower height, controller), micro-hydro (head, flow, turbine, penstock). Hybrid systems combining multiple sources.
- Practice: Design a complete off-grid power system for a community building

**Lesson 13-16: System Integration**
- Activity: Integrate structural, plumbing, electrical, and power systems into a complete building design. Coordinate systems to avoid conflicts. Create complete construction documents.
- Practice: Complete integrated design for a community building project

#### Assessment
- Design complete plumbing and electrical systems
- Design an off-grid power system with calculations
- Integrate all building systems into coordinated plans

## Grade 11: Mechanical Engineering and Advanced Systems

### Unit 5: Mechanical Engineering (Weeks 1-18)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Engine access, pump equipment, mill/turbine components, workshop tools
**Survival Relevance**: Mechanical systems power community operations — water pumping, grain milling, wood processing, and power generation

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-6: Engines and Power Transmission**
- Activity: Internal combustion engine overhaul. Steam engine principles. Power transmission: belts, chains, gears, shafts. Mechanical advantage through gear ratios. Clutches and brakes.
- Practice: Rebuild or maintain a community engine, design a power transmission system

**Lesson 7-12: Pumps, Mills, and Turbines**
- Activity: Pump types: centrifugal, piston, diaphragm, ram. Selection based on application. Mill design: grain mills, saw mills, using water or wind power. Turbine design: water turbines (Pelton, Francis, crossflow), wind turbines.
- Practice: Design and build (or repair) a pump, mill, or turbine

**Lesson 13-18: Vehicle Maintenance and Repair**
- Activity: Vehicle systems: engine, transmission, brakes, suspension, steering, electrical. Systematic diagnosis and repair. Preventive maintenance schedules. Tire repair, brake service, oil change, filter replacement.
- Practice: Perform maintenance and repairs on community vehicles

#### Assessment
- Maintain and repair engines
- Design and build mechanical systems
- Maintain community vehicles

### Unit 6: Radio, Electronics, and Advanced Metalworking (Weeks 19-34)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Electronic components, radio equipment, forge and metalworking tools
**Survival Relevance**: Communication equipment maintenance and metalworking enable community self-sufficiency in critical infrastructure

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-8: Radio and Electronics**
- Activity: Radio transceiver repair and maintenance, antenna construction, power supply design, electronic test equipment use, component-level troubleshooting. Build or repair community communication equipment.
- Practice: Repair or build communication equipment for community use

**Lesson 9-16: Advanced Metalworking**
- Activity: Forge welding, tool making (axes, knives, chisels, agricultural tools), blacksmithing projects, heat treatment for optimal properties, metal casting basics (sand casting).
- Practice: Forge tools for community use

#### Assessment
- Repair and maintain radio equipment
- Forge functional tools using proper technique
- Demonstrate component-level electronics troubleshooting

## Grade 12: Engineering Capstone

### Unit 7: Capstone Infrastructure Project (Full Year)
**Duration**: Full academic year
**Materials**: All available engineering resources
**Survival Relevance**: The capstone produces real community infrastructure

#### Structure
Each student or team designs and builds a significant infrastructure project:
- **Building**: Shelter, workshop, storage, greenhouse, or community facility
- **Water System**: Well, pump house, treatment plant, irrigation system, or water storage
- **Power System**: Solar array, wind turbine, micro-hydro, or hybrid power installation
- **Mechanical System**: Mill, forge, workshop equipment, or vehicle restoration
- **Communication System**: Radio station, antenna array, or network infrastructure

#### Requirements
- Complete engineering design with calculations
- Technical drawings (plan, elevation, section, detail)
- Materials list and procurement plan
- Construction schedule
- Build under supervision with quality control
- Testing and commissioning
- Operations and maintenance manual
- As-built documentation
- Community handover with training

#### Teaching Component
- Deliver 5 engineering/trades lessons to younger students
- Mentor intermediate band students in workshop skills
- Contribute to community engineering knowledge base

#### Assessment
- Completed infrastructure project meeting design specifications
- Documentation package (drawings, calculations, O&M manual)
- Community acceptance and handover
- Teaching delivered to younger students
