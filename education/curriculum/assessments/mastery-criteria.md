# Mastery Criteria — Band Transition Requirements

## Overview
SURVIVE OS education uses mastery-based progression, not age-based. Students advance when they demonstrate competence, not when the calendar says so. This document defines what mastery looks like at each band transition.

**Principle**: "Not yet" is not failure. It is information about what to work on next.

---

## Foundation → Building Transition (K-2 → 3-5)

### Language Arts
- Read independently at a level equivalent to mid-Grade 2
- Write complete sentences with correct capitalization and punctuation
- Tell a coherent story orally with beginning, middle, and end
- Copy text legibly in manuscript handwriting

### Mathematics
- Add and subtract two-digit numbers with regrouping
- Count by 2s, 5s, and 10s to 100
- Identify basic geometric shapes and their properties
- Tell time to the half hour
- Measure length using standard units
- Solve simple word problems with addition and subtraction

### Science & Nature
- Name and describe 20+ local plants and animals
- Describe the basic water cycle
- Explain the four seasons and what causes them
- Keep a simple nature observation journal
- Demonstrate safe behavior around fire, water, animals, and tools

### History & Community
- Explain why communities need rules and leadership
- Describe how people in different environments meet basic needs
- Tell stories of at least 3 inventions/discoveries from different cultures
- Explain why trade is important for communities
- Name 3 factors that strengthen communities and 3 that weaken them
- Participate respectfully in group decision-making

### Health & Safety
- Demonstrate proper handwashing technique
- Identify 5 dangerous situations and explain what to do
- Describe the four basic human needs
- Know when and how to get adult help in an emergency
- Name basic food groups and explain why balanced meals matter

### Social Skills
- Share materials and take turns without adult prompting
- Use words to express feelings and resolve simple conflicts
- Follow community rules and classroom routines
- Work cooperatively in a small group to complete a task
- Show empathy when a peer is upset

### Arts & Music
- Sing at least 5 community songs from memory
- Draw recognizable objects from observation
- Keep rhythm with clapping or simple percussion
- Participate in a group performance

### Practical Demonstration
- Tie at least 3 basic knots
- Identify 10 edible plants in the community garden
- Use scissors, ruler, and basic craft tools safely

## Building → Intermediate Transition (3-5 → 6-8)

### Language Arts
- Read independently at a level equivalent to mid-Grade 5
- Write a 5-paragraph essay with thesis, supporting evidence, and conclusion
- Deliver a 3-minute prepared speech to an audience
- Take notes from oral presentation and written text
- Use a dictionary and reference materials independently

### Mathematics
- Perform all four operations with whole numbers fluently
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions and decimals
- Calculate area, perimeter, and volume of basic shapes
- Read and interpret graphs, charts, and tables
- Solve multi-step word problems
- Understand percentages and their applications

### Science
- Explain the scientific method and design a simple experiment
- Describe 5 body systems and their functions
- Identify 30+ local plants and animals with basic classification
- Explain electrical circuits (series and parallel)
- Describe the rock cycle and 3 types of rocks
- Explain food webs and energy flow in ecosystems

### History
- Compare how at least 4 civilizations solved the same problem differently
- Describe major trade routes and what traveled along them
- Analyze why empires form and collapse using evidence
- Present historical information from multiple perspectives
- Explain the connection between farming and civilization
- Complete a Community Health Report analyzing their own community

### Health & First Aid
- Demonstrate wound cleaning and bandaging
- Know CPR steps (can demonstrate on a cushion)
- Explain disease transmission and prevention
- Plan a balanced meal for a family
- Identify signs that require medical attention

### Agriculture
- Plan, plant, grow, and harvest vegetables independently
- Identify and manage common garden pests
- Save seeds from at least 3 crops
- Explain crop rotation and why it matters
- Care for chickens (feeding, egg collection, coop maintenance)

### Trades & Engineering
- Use hand tools safely (saw, hammer, drill, chisel)
- Build a simple project from a plan (birdhouse, planter, shelf)
- Tie 8 essential knots from memory
- Build a basic electrical circuit
- Read a simple technical drawing

### Geography & Navigation
- Use a compass to find cardinal directions
- Read a topographic map and determine elevation
- Navigate to a destination using map and compass
- Identify the North Star and 3 constellations
- Draw an accurate map of the local area

### Communication
- Explain the electromagnetic spectrum in simple terms
- Send and receive Morse code at 5 words per minute
- Operate a Meshtastic device for basic messaging

### Arts & Culture
- Play a musical instrument at a basic level or sing in an ensemble
- Complete a visual art portfolio demonstrating growth
- Participate in a dramatic performance
- Document a community tradition in writing or art

### Practical Demonstration
- Start a fire safely using 2 methods
- Purify water using 2 methods
- Navigate to a waypoint 1 mile away using compass only
- Identify 20 useful plants (food, medicine, material)
- Build an emergency shelter from natural materials

## Intermediate → Advanced Transition (6-8 → 9-12)

### Language Arts
- Write a research paper with proper citations (5+ sources)
- Deliver a 10-minute persuasive speech with evidence
- Identify logical fallacies in arguments
- Draft formal documents (proposals, reports, letters)
- Analyze primary source documents for bias and perspective

### Mathematics
- Solve linear equations and systems of equations
- Apply the Pythagorean theorem to real problems
- Calculate probability and use statistical measures
- Perform financial calculations (interest, budgets, trade)
- Apply algebra to practical problems (dosing, structural, agricultural)

### Life Science
- Describe cell structure and function at the organelle level
- Explain genetics, heredity, and natural selection
- Identify 50+ local species with ecological roles
- Describe all major human body systems in detail
- Explain disease mechanisms and immune response

### Earth Science
- Explain plate tectonics and predict geological events
- Forecast weather using observation and basic instruments
- Describe the water cycle including groundwater systems
- Navigate by stars (identify 10 celestial objects, find latitude)
- Identify rocks and minerals with practical applications

### History
- Analyze governance systems using a consistent comparative framework
- Work with primary sources to extract and evaluate information
- Present events from multiple perspectives without bias
- Conduct independent historical research and present findings
- Draft governance proposals based on historical precedents
- Complete a Community Health Report or governance design project

### Health & Medicine
- Perform triage assessment and prioritize casualties
- Manage hemorrhage, fractures, and shock
- Recognize 20 common medical conditions
- Administer basic wound care including suturing awareness
- Design a community health intervention plan

### Economics
- Explain supply and demand with real examples
- Maintain basic accounting records (double-entry)
- Negotiate trade agreements using principled negotiation
- Design a community economic plan for 50 people
- Analyze economic systems by documented outcomes

### Agriculture
- Design a crop rotation plan for a garden
- Formulate a basic livestock ration
- Manage a production garden through a full season
- Preserve food using 3+ methods
- Calculate production economics (yield per acre, cost per unit)

### Engineering & Trades
- Read and create technical drawings (plan, elevation, section)
- Calculate structural loads for a simple building
- Install basic plumbing and electrical systems
- Repair and maintain a small engine
- Weld, braze, or solder (one or more) to functional standard

### Communication Technology
- Operate ham radio equipment on VHF/UHF
- Configure and maintain Meshtastic network nodes
- Build a functional antenna
- Troubleshoot basic electronic circuits
- Write a simple program (Python or similar)

### Practical Demonstration
- Build a structure from design through completion (minimum: shed-sized)
- Grow and process enough food to feed a family for 1 week
- Provide first aid for a simulated multi-casualty scenario
- Navigate a 5-mile course using map, compass, and stars
- Repair a community system (plumbing, electrical, mechanical, or communication)

## Assessment Process

### How Mastery Is Evaluated
1. **Portfolio review**: Student presents collected work demonstrating growth and competence
2. **Practical demonstration**: Student performs key skills under observation
3. **Oral examination**: Student explains concepts, answers questions, defends positions
4. **Community contribution**: Student demonstrates skills through real community service
5. **Teacher recommendation**: Teacher certifies readiness based on ongoing observation

### Progression Principles
- Students may advance in individual subjects at different rates
- Mastery criteria must be met, not approximated — "close enough" is not mastery for life-critical skills
- Students who don't meet criteria receive targeted support and additional practice
- There is no stigma in taking additional time — mastery matters more than speed
- Exceptional students may advance early with documentation of mastery
- Band transitions happen when ALL criteria for the new band are met, not just most
