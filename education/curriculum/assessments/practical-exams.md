# Practical Examinations — Hands-On Assessment Tasks

## Overview
Every subject in the SURVIVE OS curriculum culminates in practical demonstrations of competence, not just written tests. These examinations verify that students can actually DO what they've learned, not just describe it. Practical exams are evaluated by teachers and community practitioners who use the skills daily.

---

## Foundation Band (Grades K-2)

### Life Skills
- **Fire Safety**: Given a scenario (candle tips over, smoke in a room), demonstrate correct response
- **Water Safety**: Demonstrate what to do when you see someone near dangerous water
- **Plant Identification**: Walk through the garden and correctly name 10 edible plants
- **Tool Safety**: Demonstrate safe handling of scissors, knife, and garden tools
- **Emergency Response**: Demonstrate "stop, drop, and roll" and "get an adult" procedures

### Academic Skills
- **Reading**: Read a short passage aloud and answer comprehension questions
- **Math**: Solve 5 practical problems (measuring ingredients, counting supplies, making change)
- **Nature Observation**: Spend 15 minutes outdoors and record 10 observations in a journal
- **Storytelling**: Tell a 2-minute story with clear beginning, middle, and end

### Social Skills
- **Conflict Resolution**: Given a roleplay scenario, demonstrate the 3-step conflict resolution process
- **Cooperation**: Complete a group task that requires sharing, turn-taking, and communication
- **Community Contribution**: Demonstrate a task they perform for the community (garden chore, cleaning, helping younger child)

---

## Building Band (Grades 3-5)

### Survival Skills
- **Fire Building**: Start a fire safely using 2 methods (friction and flint/steel or matches) within 15 minutes
- **Water Purification**: Purify water using 2 methods (boiling and filtration) and explain the science
- **Shelter Construction**: Build an emergency shelter from natural materials that would protect from rain
- **Navigation**: Navigate to a waypoint 0.5 miles away using only a compass
- **Plant Identification**: Identify 20 plants (10 edible, 5 medicinal, 5 dangerous) on a nature walk
- **Knot Tying**: Tie 8 knots from memory and explain when to use each

### Agriculture
- **Garden Planning**: Design a garden plot for a given space, explaining crop placement and rotation
- **Soil Testing**: Collect and test a soil sample, interpret results, recommend amendments
- **Seed Saving**: Demonstrate proper seed collection, cleaning, and storage for 3 crops
- **Chicken Care**: Demonstrate feeding, watering, egg collection, and coop cleaning

### Trades
- **Woodworking**: Build a functional item (birdhouse, tool rack, or stool) from a simple plan
- **Electrical Circuit**: Build a working circuit with switch, light, and battery; explain how it works
- **Sewing**: Sew a functional item (bag, patch, or simple garment repair) using 2 stitch types

### History
- **Civilization Comparison**: Given 2 civilizations, compare their approaches to a common problem (food, governance, engineering) using evidence from multiple sources
- **Community Health Report**: Present a Community Health Report analyzing the community against historical collapse factors
- **Multiple Perspectives**: Describe a historical event from 2 different perspectives without favoring either

### Communication
- **Morse Code**: Send and receive a 10-word message in Morse code
- **Signal Systems**: Send a message across 100 meters using only visual signals
- **Meshtastic**: Send and receive a message using a Meshtastic device

## Intermediate Band (Grades 6-8)

### Emergency Response
- **Wound Care**: Clean, close (butterfly strips or suture awareness), and dress a simulated wound
- **Fracture Management**: Assess, splint, and document a simulated limb fracture
- **CPR**: Perform CPR on a training mannequin with correct rate, depth, and ratio
- **Triage**: Given a 5-casualty scenario, triage using START system and explain decisions
- **Choking Response**: Demonstrate Heimlich maneuver on a training mannequin

### Navigation
- **Day Navigation**: Navigate a 3-mile course using map and compass through varied terrain
- **Night Navigation**: Identify 10 celestial objects and determine direction and approximate latitude
- **Dead Reckoning**: Navigate 1 mile using pace count and compass bearing only

### Agriculture
- **Farm Design**: Design a working farm for 50 people with crop rotation, livestock integration, water system, and economics
- **Livestock Assessment**: Assess a live animal's body condition, check vital signs, identify 3 common health issues
- **Food Preservation**: Preserve a batch of food using 3 different methods and explain the science of each
- **Irrigation**: Design and install a drip irrigation system for a garden bed

### Engineering & Trades
- **Technical Drawing**: Produce a complete drawing set (plan, elevation, section) for a simple structure
- **Construction**: Build a structural element (wall frame, roof truss, or foundation form) to specification
- **Plumbing**: Install a water supply and drain for a fixture
- **Electrical**: Wire a circuit with switch, outlet, and light from a distribution panel (low-voltage training)
- **Metalwork**: Sharpen, repair, or forge a tool to functional standard

### Communication Technology
- **Radio Operation**: Make contacts on VHF/UHF using proper procedure
- **Network Troubleshooting**: Diagnose and repair a simulated network fault
- **Programming**: Write a program that solves a practical problem (inventory tracker, weather logger, or calculator)

### Economics
- **Accounting**: Maintain double-entry books for a small operation for 2 weeks with accurate balance
- **Trade Negotiation**: Complete a simulated trade negotiation with another student, reaching a fair agreement
- **Economic Analysis**: Present a cost-benefit analysis of a community investment proposal

### History
- **Primary Source Analysis**: Given an unfamiliar primary source, identify author, audience, purpose, bias, and historical context
- **Governance Debate**: Participate in a structured debate on a governance question, presenting evidence from multiple historical examples
- **Collapse Analysis**: Present a forensic analysis of a civilizational collapse with evidence, multi-factor analysis, and community recommendations
- **Governance Design**: As part of a team, draft a complete governance package (charter, justice system, economic plan) and present to evaluators

## Advanced Band (Grades 9-12)

### Medicine
- **Patient Assessment**: Perform a complete head-to-toe physical examination in under 30 minutes
- **Trauma Management**: Manage a simulated multi-trauma patient using ABCDE protocol
- **Suturing**: Close a simulated wound with appropriate suture technique
- **Medication Dosing**: Calculate correct dosages for 10 clinical scenarios without error
- **Emergency Response**: Lead a team through a mass casualty simulation

### Engineering
- **Structural Design**: Design a structure with load calculations, produce drawings, and build a scale model
- **Solar Power System**: Design, install, and commission a solar panel/battery system
- **Water System**: Design and install a gravity-fed water supply for a building
- **Engine Rebuild**: Disassemble, inspect, and reassemble a small engine to operational standard
- **Welding/Forging**: Produce a structural weld or forge a tool to specification

### Agriculture
- **Season Management**: Manage a production plot through a full growing season with documented inputs, outputs, and economics
- **Breeding Program**: Design a 10-generation livestock breeding program with genetic justification
- **Soil Analysis**: Complete a soil assessment with chemistry, biology, and management recommendations
- **Processing Line**: Run a food processing line from raw harvest through preserved product

### Communication
- **Ham Radio**: Operate on multiple bands and modes, make long-distance contacts
- **Network Deployment**: Design, build, and deploy a mesh network segment with coverage analysis
- **System Integration**: Design a multi-tier communication system (local, regional, long-range)

### Leadership
- **Project Management**: Lead a team through a significant project from planning through completion
- **Crisis Communication**: Lead communication during a simulated community crisis
- **Meeting Facilitation**: Facilitate a real community meeting with agenda, discussion, and decisions

### Economics
- **Economic Plan**: Develop a complete economic plan for a community of 500 people
- **Financial Statements**: Prepare balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow from raw data
- **Trade Policy**: Draft a trade policy with economic justification and present for critique

### History
- **Political Philosophy**: Write and defend a 3,000-word political philosophy paper engaging 4+ thinkers
- **Comparative Collapse Analysis**: Present a 5,000-word comparative analysis of collapse vs resilience
- **Community Resilience Assessment**: Deliver a comprehensive 7-component community resilience report to community leaders
- **Historiography**: Analyze a historical account, identify bias, and present from missing perspectives

## Examination Procedures

### Evaluator Requirements
- Practical exams are evaluated by at least 2 people: the teacher and a community practitioner in the relevant field
- For life-critical skills (medical, fire, structural), a qualified practitioner MUST be present
- Evaluators use standardized rubrics (see portfolio-guidelines.md for rubric templates)

### Retake Policy
- Students who do not demonstrate mastery may retake after additional practice (minimum 2 weeks)
- Retakes are not penalties — they are opportunities to demonstrate readiness
- Students receive specific feedback on what to improve before retaking
- There is no limit on retakes. Mastery is the goal, not speed.

### Safety
- All practical exams involving fire, sharp tools, electricity, heights, or medical procedures require safety supervision
- Emergency supplies (first aid kit, fire extinguisher, communication) must be available at all exam sites
- Examiners can stop any exam immediately if safety is at risk
