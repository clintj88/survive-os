# Building Band (Grades 3-5, Ages 8-11) — Mathematics

## Overview
Building Band mathematics transitions students from concrete counting and basic operations to multiplicative thinking, fractions, measurement, and data analysis. Every concept is grounded in survival applications: calculating crop yields, measuring construction materials, rationing supplies, understanding trade quantities, and scaling recipes. By Grade 5, students are fluent with all four operations, comfortable with fractions and decimals, and can apply mathematics to real community problems.

---

## Grade 3: Multiplication, Fractions, and Measurement

### Unit 1: Multiplication and Division Facts (Weeks 1-14)
**Duration**: 14 weeks
**Prerequisites**: Foundation Band — addition/subtraction fluency within 100, intro to multiplication (0, 1, 2, 5, 10 facts)
**Materials**: Arrays of objects (stones in rows), graph paper if available, multiplication table chart
**Survival Relevance**: Multiplication is essential for calculating quantities — "How many eggs from 6 chickens in a week?" "How many seedlings for 8 rows of 12?"

#### Learning Objectives
- Know all multiplication facts 0-10 from memory (automatic recall)
- Know all related division facts (if 6×7=42, then 42÷7=6)
- Understand multiplication as repeated addition and as arrays
- Understand division as sharing equally and as repeated subtraction
- Solve multiplication and division word problems

#### Teacher Notes
Fact fluency is the goal — students should be able to recall facts without counting. This takes sustained daily practice. Use games, speed drills (against yourself, not others), songs, and real-world problems. The times table is one of the most valuable tools in all of mathematics. Post it prominently. Facts for 0, 1, 2, 5, 10 should already be automatic from Foundation. Focus on 3, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9.

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-3: Review and Extend**
- Activity: Review known facts (0, 1, 2, 5, 10). Extend to 3s and 4s using arrays and skip counting.
- Practice: Daily 5-minute fact practice (flash cards, partner quizzing, games)

**Weeks 4-6: 6s, 7s, 8s, 9s**
- Activity: Teach strategies — 6×7 is the same as 5×7 + 7. 9s pattern (fingers trick, digits always add to 9). 8s are double 4s.
- Practice: Build multiplication table collaboratively on wall chart

**Weeks 7-9: Division as the Reverse**
- Activity: "If 6 rows of 8 plants = 48 plants, and you have 48 plants for 8 rows, how many per row?" Division is multiplication backward.
- Practice: Fact families (6×8=48, 8×6=48, 48÷6=8, 48÷8=6)

**Weeks 10-12: Word Problems**
- Activity: Survival word problems. "We harvest 7 baskets of apples per tree. We have 9 trees. How many baskets?" "56 fish caught today, shared equally among 8 families. How many each?"
- Practice: Write your own word problems for a partner to solve

**Weeks 13-14: Fact Fluency Assessment and Games**
- Activity: Timed personal-best challenges. Multiplication bingo. Division relay.

#### Assessment
- Recalls all facts 0-10 within 3 seconds (automatic)
- Solves division problems using multiplication knowledge
- Can solve multi-step word problems using multiplication and division

### Unit 2: Introduction to Fractions (Weeks 15-24)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Objects to divide (rope, paper, food items), fraction strips (cut paper into halves, thirds, fourths, etc.)
**Survival Relevance**: Fractions are everywhere in survival — half a ration, a quarter turn of a bolt, thirds of a harvest distributed to three groups

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand fractions as equal parts of a whole
- Read, write, and compare fractions (halves, thirds, fourths, sixths, eighths)
- Place fractions on a number line
- Understand equivalent fractions (1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6)
- Solve simple fraction word problems

#### Lessons
**Weeks 15-17: What Is a Fraction?**
- Activity: Cut a rope in half. "How many pieces? Each piece is 1/2 of the rope." Cut paper into thirds, fourths, sixths. "The bottom number tells how many equal pieces. The top number tells how many you have."
- Practice: Identify fractions of real objects. Share food items equally.

**Weeks 18-20: Comparing Fractions**
- Activity: "Which is bigger, 1/3 or 1/4?" Cut two identical strips — one into thirds, one into fourths. Compare. "More pieces means smaller pieces."
- Practice: Order fractions from smallest to largest using fraction strips

**Weeks 21-22: Number Line**
- Activity: Draw a number line from 0 to 1. Mark halves, then fourths, then eighths. "Fractions are numbers between whole numbers."

**Weeks 23-24: Equivalent Fractions**
- Activity: "Is 1/2 the same as 2/4?" Fold paper to show. "Yes! They cover the same amount."
- Practice: Find equivalent fractions using fraction strips and number lines

#### Assessment
- Can read, write, and identify fractions of real objects
- Can compare fractions with same numerator or same denominator
- Can place fractions on a number line
- Can identify equivalent fractions

### Unit 3: Area, Perimeter, and Measurement (Weeks 25-34)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Rulers, measuring tape/string, graph paper if available, garden plots
**Survival Relevance**: Area and perimeter are used daily in construction, farming, and resource planning

#### Learning Objectives
- Measure accurately in centimeters and meters
- Calculate perimeter of rectangles and irregular shapes
- Calculate area of rectangles (length × width)
- Solve real-world measurement problems
- Understand and use standard units of weight (grams, kilograms) and volume (milliliters, liters)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 25-27: Perimeter**
- Activity: Walk the perimeter of the classroom, a garden bed, a building. Measure each side. Add all sides. "Perimeter is the distance around something."
- Survival project: Calculate how much fencing needed for a garden area

**Weeks 28-30: Area**
- Activity: Tile a surface with square units (cut paper squares). Count tiles. "Area is how much surface is covered." Discover the shortcut: length × width.
- Survival project: Calculate planting area of each garden bed

**Weeks 31-32: Weight and Volume**
- Activity: Use a balance/scale. "How many grams is this apple? How many kilograms of grain in this bag?" Use measuring cups. "How many milliliters in this container?"
- Survival project: Weigh food rations. Measure water for purification.

**Weeks 33-34: Applied Measurement Problems**
- Activity: Multi-step problems. "The garden is 4m × 6m. Each plant needs 0.5m × 0.5m. How many plants fit?" "We need 3 liters of water per person per day. We have 15 people. How much water per day?"

#### Assessment
- Can measure length, weight, and volume accurately
- Can calculate perimeter by adding side lengths
- Can calculate area using length × width
- Can solve multi-step measurement word problems

### Unit 4: Time, Data, and Graphs (Weeks 35-40)
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Materials**: Clock (or drawn clock face), data collection sheets, graph paper
**Survival Relevance**: Time management, data collection, and interpretation are essential for farming schedules, resource tracking, and community planning

#### Learning Objectives
- Tell time to the minute on an analog clock
- Calculate elapsed time (start time + duration = end time)
- Collect, organize, and display data in bar graphs and pictographs
- Interpret graphs to answer questions and make decisions

#### Lessons
**Weeks 35-36: Time to the Minute**
- Activity: Review hour and half-hour from Foundation. Extend to 5-minute intervals, then individual minutes. Practice with analog clock face.
- Survival connection: "Garden watering schedule starts at 6:15 and runs for 45 minutes. When does it end?"

**Weeks 37-38: Data Collection**
- Activity: Class collects real data — egg production per day, temperature readings, rainfall amounts, plant heights. Record in organized tables.

**Weeks 39-40: Graphs**
- Activity: Create bar graphs and pictographs from collected data. Answer questions: "Which day had the most eggs? What's the trend in temperature?"
- Survival connection: "This data helps us make decisions. If egg production drops, we investigate why."

## Grade 4: Multi-Digit Operations, Fractions, and Geometry

### Unit 5: Multi-Digit Multiplication (Weeks 1-10)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Paper, manipulatives for place value
**Survival Relevance**: Multi-digit multiplication is needed for inventory (237 items × 4 storage areas), construction (boards of specific lengths), and resource planning

#### Learning Objectives
- Multiply 2-digit by 1-digit numbers (37 × 6)
- Multiply 2-digit by 2-digit numbers (24 × 13)
- Use estimation to check reasonableness (round first, then multiply)
- Solve multi-step word problems requiring multiplication

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-3: 2-digit × 1-digit**
- Activity: Area model — draw a rectangle. Split into tens and ones. 37 × 6 = (30 × 6) + (7 × 6) = 180 + 42 = 222. Then teach standard algorithm.

**Weeks 4-6: 2-digit × 2-digit**
- Activity: Area model with four sections. 24 × 13 = (20×10) + (20×3) + (4×10) + (4×3). Then standard algorithm (lattice method as alternative).

**Weeks 7-8: Estimation**
- Activity: "Before you calculate, estimate. 37 × 6 ≈ 40 × 6 = 240. Your answer should be close to 240. If you got 2,220, something went wrong."

**Weeks 9-10: Applied Problems**
- Activity: Community math problems. "We need 48 fence posts. Each costs 3 trade units. Total cost?" "The well produces 125 liters per hour. How much in 8 hours?"

### Unit 6: Long Division (Weeks 11-20)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Paper, manipulatives
**Survival Relevance**: Division is sharing — dividing resources, rationing supplies, distributing harvests equally

#### Learning Objectives
- Divide 2-3 digit numbers by 1-digit divisors (456 ÷ 8)
- Understand remainders and what they mean in context
- Check division with multiplication
- Solve word problems requiring division

#### Lessons
**Weeks 11-14: Division Algorithm**
- Activity: Teach divide-multiply-subtract-bring down using place value understanding. Start with no remainder, then introduce remainders.

**Weeks 15-17: Remainders in Context**
- Activity: "28 apples ÷ 6 baskets = 4 remainder 4. What do we do with the remaining 4 apples?" Context matters — sometimes round up (need 5 baskets), sometimes the remainder is discarded, sometimes it's a fraction.

**Weeks 18-20: Applied Division**
- Activity: "We harvested 372 potatoes. Distribute equally to 12 families. How many each?"

### Unit 7: Fraction Operations and Decimals (Weeks 21-30)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Fraction strips, number lines, money (if community uses currency)
**Survival Relevance**: Fraction and decimal operations are needed for recipe scaling, measurement, and material calculations

#### Learning Objectives
- Add and subtract fractions with like denominators (3/8 + 2/8 = 5/8)
- Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions
- Understand decimals as another way to write fractions (tenths, hundredths)
- Add and subtract decimals
- Relate fractions and decimals to money concepts

#### Lessons
**Weeks 21-23: Adding and Subtracting Like-Denominator Fractions**
- Activity: "If you eat 2/8 of the bread and then 3/8 more, how much did you eat?" Use fraction strips — combine the pieces.

**Weeks 24-25: Mixed Numbers**
- Activity: "1 and 3/4 — that means one whole and three-quarters." Practice converting between mixed numbers and improper fractions.

**Weeks 26-28: Decimals Introduction**
- Activity: "A decimal is a fraction with a denominator of 10, 100, etc. 0.5 = 5/10 = 1/2. 0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4." Use a ruler marked in tenths.

**Weeks 29-30: Decimal Operations**
- Activity: Add and subtract decimals by lining up decimal points. Relate to money: $3.45 + $2.30.

### Unit 8: Angles, Shapes, and Coordinate Grids (Weeks 31-40)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Protractor (or made from paper), graph paper, ruler, compass (drawing tool)
**Survival Relevance**: Geometry is essential for construction (right angles, structural shapes), navigation (bearings, coordinates), and mapping

#### Learning Objectives
- Measure and draw angles (right, acute, obtuse)
- Classify triangles and quadrilaterals by their properties
- Plot points on a coordinate grid
- Find locations using coordinates (connects to navigation)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 31-33: Angles**
- Activity: Find right angles in the classroom (corners of paper, door frames). "A right angle is 90°. Less than 90° is acute. More is obtuse." Measure angles with a protractor.

**Weeks 34-36: Shape Properties**
- Activity: Classify shapes by sides, angles, and parallel lines. "Why do builders use triangles? Because they're the strongest shape — they don't collapse."

**Weeks 37-40: Coordinate Grids**
- Activity: Plot points on a grid. Find a "treasure" at given coordinates. Create a map of the classroom using coordinates.
- Connection: This directly leads to map reading and GPS concepts.

## Grade 5: Fractions, Decimals, Percentages, and Applied Math

### Unit 9: Advanced Fraction Operations (Weeks 1-10)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Fraction strips, paper
**Survival Relevance**: Real-world fractions rarely have the same denominator — recipe scaling, material cutting, and resource division require unlike-denominator operations

#### Learning Objectives
- Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators (find common denominator)
- Multiply fractions (3/4 × 2/3)
- Divide fractions (basic concept — "how many halves in 3?")
- Solve complex fraction word problems

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-4: Unlike Denominators**
- Activity: "You can't add 1/3 + 1/4 directly because the pieces are different sizes. You need to make them the same size." Find common denominators. 1/3 = 4/12, 1/4 = 3/12, so 1/3 + 1/4 = 7/12.

**Weeks 5-7: Multiplying Fractions**
- Activity: "1/2 of 3/4 means half of three-quarters." Fold paper to show. Numerator × numerator, denominator × denominator. 1/2 × 3/4 = 3/8.

**Weeks 8-10: Division and Applied Problems**
- Activity: "How many 1/4-cup servings in 3 cups?" 3 ÷ 1/4 = 12. "You have 2/3 of a bag of seed. You need 1/6 bag per row. How many rows?" 2/3 ÷ 1/6 = 4.

### Unit 10: Decimals, Percentages, and Conversions (Weeks 11-20)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Calculator (if available), measuring tools
**Survival Relevance**: Percentages are used in trade (discounts, taxes), agriculture (germination rates, yield percentages), and health (survival rates, dosages)

#### Learning Objectives
- Multiply and divide decimals
- Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Calculate percentages of quantities (25% of 200)
- Apply percentages to real community scenarios

#### Lessons
**Weeks 11-14: Decimal Operations**
- Activity: Multiply: 3.4 × 2.1 (multiply as whole numbers, count decimal places). Divide: 14.4 ÷ 1.2. Real context: "Each person needs 2.5 liters of water. 14 people. Total?"

**Weeks 15-17: Fraction-Decimal-Percentage Conversions**
- Activity: 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%. 1/4 = 0.25 = 25%. 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%. Build a conversion chart.

**Weeks 18-20: Percentage Applications**
- Activity: "Germination rate is 80%. We planted 150 seeds. How many should sprout?" "Trade deal: 15% bonus if we deliver early. Normal payment is 200 units."

---

### Unit 11: Volume, Unit Conversions, and Order of Operations (Weeks 21-32)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: 3D shapes, containers, rulers
**Survival Relevance**: Volume calculations for water storage, construction, and material planning. Unit conversions for coordinating with other communities that may use different systems.

#### Learning Objectives
- Calculate volume of rectangular prisms (length × width × height)
- Convert between units within metric system (mm↔cm↔m, mL↔L, g↔kg)
- Apply order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)
- Understand negative numbers on a number line (above/below zero temperature)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 21-24: Volume**
- Activity: Build rectangular prisms from unit cubes. Count cubes. Discover formula: l × w × h.
- Survival project: "How many liters does our water tank hold? Measure and calculate."

**Weeks 25-28: Unit Conversions**
- Activity: "1 meter = 100 centimeters = 1000 millimeters. 1 kilogram = 1000 grams. 1 liter = 1000 milliliters." Practice converting with real measurements.

**Weeks 29-30: Order of Operations**
- Activity: "Does 3 + 4 × 2 = 14 or 11?" Teach PEMDAS. Practice with multi-operation problems.

**Weeks 31-32: Negative Numbers**
- Activity: Number line extending below zero. Temperature on cold mornings. Debt in trade. "Negative numbers are real — they represent below zero, owing money, or going backward."

---

### Unit 12: Applied Mathematics Capstone (Weeks 33-40)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: All measurement tools, real community data
**Survival Relevance**: This unit is entirely applied — students use math to solve real community problems

#### Learning Objectives
- Apply multiple math skills to a real community project
- Present mathematical findings to the community
- Demonstrate that math is a tool, not just a school subject

#### Projects (choose 2-3)
**Crop Yield Calculator**: Measure garden plots (area). Count plants. Track harvest weights. Calculate yield per square meter. Compare plots. Recommend best practices.

**Construction Math**: Help a trades team member calculate materials for a project. Board lengths, angles, quantities, waste estimates.

**Ration Planning**: Given community food stores and population, calculate daily rations for 30 days. Factor in different needs (children vs. adults, sick vs. healthy).

**Trade Math**: Calculate fair trade ratios between communities. "If we trade 50 kg of grain for 20 kg of dried fish, what's the per-kilogram exchange rate?"

#### Assessment (Grade 5 Overall)
- All multiplication and division facts automatic
- Can add/subtract/multiply fractions with unlike denominators
- Can perform decimal operations
- Can calculate percentages
- Can calculate area, perimeter, and volume
- Can solve multi-step word problems involving multiple operations
- Completed a real-world math application project
