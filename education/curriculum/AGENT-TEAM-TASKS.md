### Format Requirements
**Every new file must follow this format**: units with duration, materials, survival relevance, learning objectives, teacher notes, detailed lessons, and assessments. Keep content practical and tied to survival scenarios.

### History Curriculum — CRITICAL INSTRUCTIONS
History is taught **thematically, NOT as national narratives**. Read `history-framework/HISTORY-PHILOSOPHY.md` before building ANY history content. The 7 core commitments are non-negotiable:
1. No national framing — organize by themes, not nations
2. Multiple perspectives always — every event from at least two viewpoints
3. Honest about uncertainty — say "we don't know" when appropriate
4. No ideology as truth — present all systems by their documented outcomes
5. Atrocities acknowledged, not weaponized — present as systemic conditions any group can fall into
6. No hero worship — analyze systems, not idolize individuals
7. Religion is cultural history, not theology — describe beliefs and impacts, never endorse or condemn

The history-framework files contain the COMPLETE thematic structure, case studies, and key questions for each band. When building the `history.md` files in each band directory, expand these frameworks into the full unit format with detailed lesson plans, activities, materials lists, and assessments — matching the format of the language-arts and mathematics examples.

### Foundation Band (Grades K-2) — Files Needed:

#### `foundation/health-safety.md`
3-year curriculum covering:
- Year 1: Personal hygiene (handwashing, dental care, bathing), body safety, stranger awareness, fire safety, water safety, poison awareness (don't eat unknown plants/berries), basic nutrition (food groups using available foods), sleep importance
- Year 2: Basic first aid (cuts, scrapes, burns, bee stings), when to get adult help, illness prevention (covering coughs, staying home when sick), emotional health (naming feelings, calming strategies, asking for help), sun safety, animal safety (livestock, wildlife)
- Year 3: CPR awareness (not performance — know what it is), choking response (know to get help), infection signs (red, swollen, hot = tell an adult), basic wound care (clean, cover, tell someone), nutrition planning (helping plan a balanced meal), puberty introduction (age-appropriate body changes preview)

#### `foundation/history.md` ← HISTORY SPECIALIST
Expand `history-framework/foundation-history.md` into full lesson-plan format.
3-year thematic curriculum covering:
- Year 1 (Our Community, Our Story): Who we are, community roles, our community's history, community rules and why they exist. How people meet basic needs (food, water, shelter, safety) in different environments. Long ago vs today, the invention chain, what happens when knowledge is lost.
- Year 2 (People and Places): Different environments different lives (Arctic, desert, tropical, island peoples). How food changed everything (agricultural revolution — multiple origins). How people communicate (oral tradition, writing invention, printing, messaging across distance).
- Year 3 (How Communities Work): Rules, leaders, and fairness (comparing governance methods). Trade and how communities help each other (trade routes, fair exchange). What lasts and what doesn't (collapse factors, resilience factors, Easter Island story, community strength recipe).
- **CRITICAL**: Follow the 7 commitments from HISTORY-PHILOSOPHY.md. Present all cultures with equal respect. Multiple perspectives on every topic. No hero worship or villain narratives. Connect every unit to "what does this mean for OUR community?"

#### `foundation/social-skills.md`
3-year curriculum covering:
- Year 1: Sharing, taking turns, using kind words, listening when others speak, following community rules, understanding roles (everyone has jobs), cooperation games, resolving small conflicts with words
- Year 2: Empathy (understanding others' feelings), teamwork projects, community helpers (who does what and why), responsibility (completing assigned tasks), honesty and trust, friendship skills
- Year 3: Leadership basics (leading a small group activity), teaching others (peer tutoring), community meetings (how to participate respectfully), fairness and justice concepts, cultural awareness (different families/backgrounds), conflict mediation (help two others resolve a dispute)

#### `foundation/arts-music.md`
3-year curriculum covering:
- Year 1: Rhythm and singing (clapping patterns, community songs, echo singing), drawing from observation (nature sketching), color mixing with natural pigments (berries, charcoal, clay), storytelling through pictures, movement and dance
- Year 2: Simple instruments (drums from containers, flutes from reeds/bamboo, string instruments from boxes), painting and printmaking with natural materials, group performance (songs, short plays), pattern design (weaving patterns, textile design introduction), folk tales and oral tradition
- Year 3: Song writing and composition, sculpture with clay and found materials, community mural project, dramatic performance (full short play), instrument building workshop, art as historical record (documenting community life through art)
- Survival relevance: Arts preserve culture, maintain morale, and provide skills for trade goods (pottery, textiles, instruments)
