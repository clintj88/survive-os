# Foundation Band (Grades K-2, Ages 5-8) — Science & Nature

## Overview
Science at this level is nature observation, classification, and curiosity. Every lesson happens outdoors whenever possible. Children learn to observe carefully, ask questions, test ideas, and record findings. This builds the scientific thinking that later becomes formal biology, chemistry, and physics.

## Year 1: Our World

### Unit 1: Observation & Senses (Weeks 1-6)
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Materials**: Journals (any paper), charcoal/pencil for drawing
**Survival Relevance**: Careful observation is the foundation of foraging, tracking, weather reading, and threat detection

#### Learning Objectives
- Use five senses to observe and describe objects and environments
- Record observations through drawings and simple words
- Sort and classify objects by observable properties (color, size, shape, texture, weight)
- Begin a nature journal

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Sense Walk**
- Activity: Walk through community area. Stop at stations. "What do you see? Hear? Smell? Feel?" Record in journal.
- Practice: Describe objects using 3+ sensory details

**Lesson 3-4: Sorting & Classifying**
- Activity: Collect 20 natural objects (rocks, leaves, seeds, feathers). Sort by property. Re-sort by a different property.
- Key concept: The same objects can be grouped different ways depending on the property you choose.

**Lesson 5-6: Nature Journal Introduction**
- Activity: Draw one plant or animal per week. Label parts you can name. Write 1-2 observations.
- This journal continues throughout all 3 years.

### Unit 2: Plants (Weeks 7-14)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Seeds (bean, sunflower, or whatever is available), soil, water, containers
**Survival Relevance**: Directly connects to agriculture — children who understand plants become better farmers

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify parts of a plant (roots, stem, leaves, flower, fruit, seed)
- Understand what plants need to grow (water, light, soil, air)
- Observe and document a plant growing from seed
- Understand the basic plant life cycle (seed → sprout → plant → flower → fruit → seed)
- Identify 5+ edible plants in the local environment

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Plant Parts**
- Activity: Pull up a complete plant (weed is fine). Identify and label each part. Discuss what each part does.
- Practice: Draw and label a plant in journal

**Lesson 3-6: Growing Experiment**
- Activity: Plant bean seeds in 4 containers. Give all water and soil. One gets light, one stays in dark. One gets water, one doesn't. Observe daily for 3 weeks.
- Key concept: Plants need water AND light to grow
- Practice: Measure and record growth. Draw daily.

**Lesson 7-8: Life Cycle & Edible Plants**
- Activity: Walk with agriculture team member to identify edible plants in community area. Compare to dangerous look-alikes.
- Practice: Draw life cycle. Create "safe to eat" field guide page.
- Survival link: Connect directly to the knowledge base foraging entries

### Unit 3: Animals (Weeks 15-22)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Observation journals, magnifying glass if available
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding animal behavior aids in hunting, trapping, livestock management, and avoiding dangerous species

#### Learning Objectives
- Classify animals: mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish, insects
- Describe what animals need to survive (food, water, shelter, air)
- Observe and document animal behavior (community livestock, wild birds, insects)
- Understand basic food chains (sun → plant → herbivore → predator)
- Identify 5+ local animal tracks and signs

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Animal Groups**
- Activity: Sort animal pictures/drawings by type. What features define each group? (Fur/feathers/scales, warm/cold blood, eggs/live birth)

**Lesson 3-4: Needs of Animals**
- Activity: Compare to plant needs. What do animals need that plants don't? (Food — animals can't make their own)
- Practice: Design an ideal habitat for a chosen animal

**Lesson 5-6: Observation Week**
- Activity: Pick one animal to observe for a week (chicken, dog, wild bird, ant colony). Record behavior daily in journal.
- Questions: When does it eat? Sleep? Move? What does it eat? Where does it shelter?

**Lesson 7-8: Food Chains**
- Activity: Build food chains with string and cards. Sun → grass → rabbit → hawk. What happens if one link is removed?
- Survival link: "We are part of food chains too. Understanding them helps us find food and protect our livestock."

### Unit 4: Weather & Seasons (Weeks 23-32)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Thermometer (if available), rain gauge (any straight container), wind vane (feather on stick)
**Survival Relevance**: Weather prediction is critical for agriculture, travel safety, and disaster preparedness

#### Learning Objectives
- Describe daily weather (sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, snowy, hot, cold)
- Record weather observations daily for 8+ weeks
- Identify cloud types: cumulus, stratus, cumulonimbus (use kid-friendly names: puffy, blanket, storm)
- Understand the four seasons and what changes (temperature, daylight, plant behavior, animal behavior)
- Understand the water cycle at a basic level (evaporation, condensation, precipitation)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Weather Words & Daily Observation**
- Activity: Create a class weather station. Record temperature (warm/cool/cold or thermometer), cloud type, precipitation, wind each morning. Use symbols on a chart.
- This continues daily for the remainder of the unit.

**Lesson 3-4: Clouds**
- Activity: Cloud watching. Lie on backs outside. Identify types. "Puffy clouds = nice weather. Blanket clouds = might rain. Tall dark clouds = storm coming."
- Practice: Draw cloud types in journal. Predict tomorrow's weather based on today's clouds.

**Lesson 5-6: Water Cycle**
- Activity: Boil water, hold cold plate over steam, watch drops form and fall. "This is what the sky does!"
- Practice: Draw and label the water cycle (evaporation from lake, clouds form, rain falls, water flows back to lake)

**Lesson 7-10: Seasons**
- Activity: Track changes month by month. When do trees bud? When do leaves change? When is the first frost? When are days longest/shortest?
- Survival link: Connect directly to the planting calendar. "Why do we plant beans in spring but not winter?"

### Unit 5: Earth Materials (Weeks 33-38)
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Materials**: Rock samples, soil samples, sand, clay, water
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding soil for agriculture, rocks for construction, clay for pottery, sand for filtration

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify three types of rock: ignite that is hard and crystally, sedimentary that has layers, metamorphic that is swirled/banded (simplified)
- Describe soil components (sand, silt, clay, organic matter)
- Understand that soil type affects plant growth
- Understand that water shapes the land (erosion)

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Rock Study**
- Activity: Collect 10 different rocks. Sort by hardness (scratch test), color, texture, crystal presence.
- Practice: Which rocks are best for tools? For building? For sharpening?

**Lesson 3-4: Soil Study**
- Activity: Collect soil from 3 different locations. Feel texture (sandy, silty, clay). Jar test: put soil in water, shake, let settle — layers show sand/silt/clay proportions.
- Survival link: "Which soil grows the best plants? Let's test!" (links to agriculture)

**Lesson 5-6: Water & Erosion**
- Activity: Pour water over a dirt mound on a tray. Observe channels forming. Add "plants" (grass or sticks). Pour again. Does erosion decrease?
- Key concept: Plants prevent erosion. This is why we don't clear-cut hillsides.

## Year 2: How Things Work

### Unit 6: Forces & Motion (Weeks 1-8)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Ramps, balls, toy vehicles, string, weights
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding forces underpins construction, tool use, and transportation

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand push and pull as forces
- Observe that heavier objects need more force to move
- Understand that smooth surfaces have less friction than rough ones
- Experiment with ramps and inclined planes
- Observe gravity ("everything falls down")

---

### Unit 7: Sound & Light (Weeks 9-16)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Rubber bands, containers, mirrors, flashlights (if available)
**Survival Relevance**: Signaling (light and sound), communication, understanding your environment

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand that sound is caused by vibration
- Observe that sound travels through air, water, and solids
- Understand that light travels in straight lines
- Observe reflection and shadows
- Understand that we see objects because light bounces off them into our eyes

---

### Unit 8: Water in Our Lives (Weeks 17-24)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Containers, filters, heat source
**Survival Relevance**: Directly connected to water purification — the #1 survival skill

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify three states of water (solid, liquid, gas) and what causes changes (heating, cooling)
- Understand why clean water matters for health
- Build a simple water filter and test it
- Understand that boiling kills germs in water
- Trace the community water supply from source to use

#### Key Project
- Build a charcoal/sand water filter (connects directly to knowledge base entry)
- Visit community water treatment setup and understand each step

## Year 3: Living Systems

### Unit 9: Habitats & Ecosystems (Weeks 1-10)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding ecosystems helps with sustainable hunting, farming, and resource management

#### Learning Objectives
- Define habitat and what makes a habitat suitable for different organisms
- Identify local habitats (forest, field, wetland, garden) and organisms in each
- Understand predator/prey relationships
- Understand that removing one species affects others (ecosystem balance)
- Recognize 15+ local plants and 10+ local animals by name

---

### Unit 10: Human Body Basics (Weeks 11-20)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Survival Relevance**: Foundation for health education and medical apprenticeship track

#### Learning Objectives
- Name major body systems: skeletal, muscular, digestive, respiratory, circulatory
- Understand basic function of each system (bones hold you up, muscles move you, stomach digests food, lungs breathe, heart pumps blood)
- Understand basic nutrition: why we need water, protein, carbohydrates, vitamins
- Understand germs and how illness spreads (hand washing, clean water, covering coughs)
- Know basic first aid: cuts (clean and cover), burns (cool water), broken bone (don't move it, get help)

---

### Unit 11: Simple Machines (Weeks 21-30)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Levers, ramps, pulleys, wheels, screws from environment
**Survival Relevance**: Every tool and construction technique uses simple machines

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify six simple machines: lever, inclined plane, wedge, screw, wheel and axle, pulley
- Find examples of each in the community
- Understand that machines make work easier by trading force for distance
- Build a simple machine to solve a problem (lever to lift heavy rock, ramp to load cart, pulley to raise bucket from well)

#### Key Project
- Identify all simple machines used in community infrastructure
- Design and build a solution to a real community problem using simple machines
