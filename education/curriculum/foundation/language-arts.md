# Foundation Band (Grades K-2, Ages 5-8) — Language Arts

## Overview
Foundation literacy is the single most important educational outcome. A child who can read can teach themselves everything else. This curriculum takes students from letter recognition through independent reading of simple texts, and from dictation through writing simple sentences and stories.

## Year 1 (Kindergarten Level)

### Unit 1: Oral Language & Listening (Weeks 1-6)
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Materials**: None required
**Survival Relevance**: Clear communication prevents accidents, enables cooperation, and preserves oral knowledge traditions

#### Learning Objectives
- Listen to and follow two-step verbal instructions
- Speak in complete sentences
- Retell a simple story in correct sequence
- Learn and recite community safety rules

#### Teacher Notes
Many children in a post-crisis environment may have experienced trauma. Oral language activities provide safe social interaction. Use circle time, partner sharing, and storytelling. Avoid putting shy children on the spot — let them warm up at their own pace.

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Listening Games**
- Activity: Simon Says, telephone game, follow-the-leader with verbal instructions
- Key Concepts: Listening, following directions, taking turns

**Lesson 3-4: Show and Tell**
- Activity: Each child brings or describes an object and speaks 2-3 sentences about it
- Key Concepts: Speaking in complete sentences, descriptive language

**Lesson 5-6: Story Circle**
- Activity: Teacher tells a short story. Children retell in their own words. Group builds a collaborative story where each child adds one sentence.
- Key Concepts: Narrative sequence (beginning, middle, end), memory, creativity

#### Assessment
- Can follow a two-step direction without repetition
- Can speak in complete sentences about a familiar topic
- Can retell a 3-event story in correct order

### Unit 2: Phonemic Awareness (Weeks 7-14)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: None required (voice only)
**Survival Relevance**: Foundation for all reading — without phonemic awareness, reading instruction fails

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify and produce rhyming words
- Segment words into syllables by clapping
- Identify beginning sounds in words
- Blend two-three sounds into words orally

#### Teacher Notes
This is AUDITORY only — no letters yet. Children must hear the sound structure of language before mapping sounds to letters. Do 10-15 minutes daily. Make it playful — songs, chants, and games.

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Rhyme Time**
- Activity: Read/recite rhyming songs and stories. "Does cat rhyme with hat? Does cat rhyme with dog?" Children generate rhyming words.
- Practice: Rhyming word chains (cat-bat-hat-mat-sat)

**Lesson 3-4: Syllable Clapping**
- Activity: Clap the beats in names and common words. "Wa-ter" (2 claps). "But-ter-fly" (3 claps).
- Practice: Sort picture cards by syllable count

**Lesson 5-6: Beginning Sounds**
- Activity: "What sound does 'mmmmoon' start with?" Emphasize first sound. Sort objects by beginning sound.
- Practice: "I spy something that starts with /s/"

**Lesson 7-8: Oral Blending**
- Activity: Teacher says sounds slowly: "/c/ /a/ /t/ — what word?" Children blend to identify word.
- Practice: Sound-by-sound blending games with 3-sound words

#### Assessment
- Can produce 3 rhyming words for a given word
- Can clap syllables in 2-3 syllable words correctly
- Can identify beginning sound in spoken words
- Can blend 3 spoken sounds into a word

### Unit 3: Letter Knowledge (Weeks 15-30)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Sand/dirt tray, sticks for writing, charcoal, any paper available
**Survival Relevance**: The alphabet is the code that unlocks all written knowledge

#### Learning Objectives
- Recognize and name all 26 uppercase letters
- Recognize and name all 26 lowercase letters
- Write all 26 letters (uppercase and lowercase)
- Associate each letter with its most common sound
- Write own first name

#### Teacher Notes
Teach 2 letters per week. Start with high-utility letters: S, A, T, P, I, N (these make the most early words). Avoid teaching similar-looking letters back-to-back (b/d, p/q). Use multi-sensory methods: trace in sand, form with sticks, write with charcoal, say the sound while writing.

#### Lessons
**Teaching sequence (2 letters per week):**
Weeks 15-16: S, A | Weeks 17-18: T, P | Weeks 19-20: I, N
Weeks 21-22: C, K | Weeks 23-24: E, H | Weeks 25-26: R, M
Weeks 27-28: D, G | Weeks 29-30: O, U, L, F, B, J, W, V, Y, Z, X, Q

**For each letter pair:**
- Day 1: Introduce letter shape, name, and sound. Trace in sand. Find in environment.
- Day 2: Practice writing. Identify words that start with the sound.
- Day 3: Review previous letters + new. Sort objects by beginning letter.
- Day 4: Write letters from memory. Begin simple letter-sound matching.
- Day 5: Assessment/review. Letter recognition speed drill.

#### Assessment
- Can name all 26 letters (upper and lowercase) with 90%+ accuracy
- Can produce the common sound for each letter
- Can write all letters legibly
- Can write first name independently

### Unit 4: Early Reading — CVC Words (Weeks 31-40)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Letter cards (hand-made), simple word lists, any available books
**Survival Relevance**: First independent reading — children can now decode signs, labels, and simple instructions

#### Learning Objectives
- Blend consonant-vowel-consonant (CVC) words: cat, sit, hop, red, bug
- Read 20+ sight words (the, is, a, I, and, to, we, he, she, it, on, in, at, up, go, no, my, do, so, an)
- Read simple sentences with CVC words and sight words
- Demonstrate left-to-right, top-to-bottom reading directionality

#### Teacher Notes
This is where reading clicks. Children who struggled with letters may suddenly take off, or may need extra time. Differentiate — let advanced readers move to longer texts while struggling readers get more CVC practice. NEVER shame a struggling reader. Pair stronger and weaker readers as buddies.

#### Lessons
**Lesson 1-2: Blending CVC Words**
- Activity: Sound boxes — draw three boxes, one per sound. Place letter in each box, slide finger under boxes while blending. "/s/... /a/... /t/... sat!"
- Practice: Build and read CVC word families (-at, -it, -op, -ed, -ug)

**Lesson 3-4: Word Families**
- Activity: Change the beginning letter to make new words. "If this is 'cat,' what happens when I change c to b?" 
- Practice: Word family flip books (made from folded paper)

**Lesson 5-6: Sight Words**
- Activity: Flash cards, word wall, "find the word" scavenger hunts
- Practice: 3-5 new sight words per week, review all previously learned

**Lesson 7-10: Sentence Reading**
- Activity: Read simple sentences combining CVC words and sight words. "The cat is on the mat." Progress to simple stories.
- Practice: Daily partner reading, echo reading (teacher reads, student repeats), choral reading (group reads together)

#### Assessment
- Can read 30+ CVC words accurately
- Can read 20+ sight words automatically (within 2 seconds)
- Can read a simple 3-5 sentence passage with 90%+ accuracy
- Demonstrates understanding by answering "who/what/where" questions about text

## Year 2 (1st Grade Level)

### Unit 5: Consonant Blends & Digraphs (Weeks 1-8)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Letter cards, word lists, simple texts
**Survival Relevance**: Unlocks reading of more complex survival-relevant words (plant, fresh, sharp, thick)

#### Learning Objectives
- Read words with initial blends (bl, br, cl, cr, dr, fl, fr, gl, gr, pl, pr, sc, sk, sl, sm, sn, sp, st, sw, tr)
- Read words with digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh, ck)
- Read words with final blends (nd, nk, nt, mp, lt, ft, sk, st)
- Expand sight word vocabulary to 50+ words

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-3: Initial Blends**
- Teach in groups: s-blends (st, sp, sn, sk, sl, sm, sw, sc), l-blends (bl, cl, fl, gl, pl, sl), r-blends (br, cr, dr, fr, gr, pr, tr)
- Activity: Blend cards — combine blend card with word ending card (st + op = stop)

**Weeks 4-5: Digraphs**
- Teach: sh (ship), ch (chin), th (this, thin), wh (when), ck (back)
- Key concept: Two letters make ONE new sound

**Weeks 6-8: Final Blends & Mixed Practice**
- Teach final blends in context of real words
- Mixed reading practice with CVC, blends, and digraphs together

---

### Unit 6: Long Vowels & Silent E (Weeks 9-14)
**Duration**: 6 weeks
**Materials**: Word cards, texts
**Survival Relevance**: Dramatically expands readable vocabulary (fire, water, stone, pine, rope, bone)

#### Learning Objectives
- Read CVCe (silent e) words: make, like, hope, cute, here
- Understand the "silent e makes the vowel say its name" rule
- Read common vowel teams: ee, ea, ai, ay, oa, ow
- Read simple multi-paragraph texts with comprehension

#### Lessons
**Weeks 9-11: Silent E Pattern**
- Activity: Magic E — show how adding e changes the word (can→cane, hop→hope, kit→kite)
- Practice: Sort words into short vowel vs long vowel with silent e

**Weeks 12-14: Vowel Teams**
- Teach: ee/ea (tree, read), ai/ay (rain, play), oa/ow (boat, show)
- Rule: "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking" (simplified, with exceptions noted)

### Unit 7: Reading Fluency & Comprehension (Weeks 15-30)
**Duration**: 16 weeks
**Materials**: Any available books, hand-written stories, community-produced texts
**Survival Relevance**: Fluent readers can independently learn from written survival materials, instructions, and knowledge base entries

#### Learning Objectives
- Read grade-level text at 40-60 words per minute with expression
- Answer who, what, where, when, why questions about text
- Make predictions before and during reading
- Retell stories including main idea and key details
- Read and follow simple written instructions (recipes, directions)
- Distinguish fiction from non-fiction

#### Lessons
**Daily structure (30-40 minutes):**
- 10 min: Guided reading with teacher (small group, leveled texts)
- 10 min: Independent reading practice
- 10 min: Comprehension discussion or written response
- 5 min: Read-aloud by teacher (model fluent reading with harder texts)

**Comprehension strategies taught:**
- Predicting: "What do you think will happen next? Why?"
- Questioning: "What questions do you have about this?"
- Connecting: "Does this remind you of anything?"
- Summarizing: "Tell me the most important parts"
- Visualizing: "Draw what you see in your mind"

---

### Unit 8: Writing — Sentences to Paragraphs (Weeks 15-40, parallel with reading)
**Duration**: 26 weeks (runs alongside reading units)
**Materials**: Any writing surface and implement
**Survival Relevance**: Written communication preserves knowledge, enables record-keeping, and supports governance

#### Learning Objectives
- Write complete sentences with capital letter and end punctuation
- Write 3-5 sentence paragraphs on a topic
- Write a personal narrative (true story from their life)
- Write a simple informational text (describe an animal, a process)
- Write a letter or message to another person
- Spell 100+ common words correctly
- Use invented/phonetic spelling for unknown words (encouraged, not penalized)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 15-20: Sentence Writing**
- Sentence starters: "I like... I can... I see... The ___ is ___."
- Expand sentences: Add "when, where, why" details

**Weeks 21-30: Paragraph Writing**
- Topic sentence + 2-3 supporting sentences + closing sentence
- Genres: personal story, description, how-to

**Weeks 31-40: Extended Writing**
- Multi-paragraph pieces (beginning, middle, end)
- Writing process: plan → draft → share → revise → publish (read to class or post on community board)

## Year 3 (2nd Grade Level)

### Unit 9: Advanced Phonics & Word Study (Weeks 1-15)
**Duration**: 15 weeks
**Materials**: Word lists, texts, dictionary if available

#### Learning Objectives
- Read words with r-controlled vowels (ar, er, ir, or, ur)
- Read words with diphthongs (oi/oy, ou/ow)
- Read common prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, dis-) and suffixes (-ing, -ed, -er, -est, -ly, -ful, -less)
- Read compound words and contractions
- Read multi-syllable words by breaking into parts
- Sight word vocabulary reaches 200+ words

---

### Unit 10: Reading Comprehension & Research (Weeks 1-40, parallel)
**Duration**: Full year
**Materials**: Any available texts — fiction, non-fiction, survival manuals, community records

#### Learning Objectives
- Read chapter books independently
- Identify main idea and supporting details in non-fiction
- Compare and contrast two texts on the same topic
- Use a table of contents and index to find information
- Take simple notes from a text (3-5 key facts)
- Read and comprehend survival knowledge base entries appropriate for age
- Read 70-90 words per minute with comprehension

---

### Unit 11: Writing for Purpose (Weeks 1-40, parallel)
**Duration**: Full year
**Materials**: Writing supplies

#### Learning Objectives
- Write multi-paragraph narrative, informational, and opinion pieces
- Write a simple report based on research (reading about a topic and summarizing findings)
- Write letters and messages for real purposes (community communication)
- Write journal entries documenting observations (weather, garden, animals)
- Use correct capitalization, periods, question marks, exclamation points, commas in a list
- Spell grade-level words correctly, use dictionary/word wall for unknown words
- Handwriting is legible and efficient

#### Survival-Relevant Writing Projects
- Write instructions for a task they know (starting a fire, planting seeds, purifying water)
- Write observations in a nature/weather journal
- Write a letter to another community member
- Document a community event for the historical record
- Create a "field guide" page for a local plant or animal
