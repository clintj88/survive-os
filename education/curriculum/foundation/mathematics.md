# Foundation Band (Grades K-2, Ages 5-8) — Mathematics

## Overview
Mathematical thinking is essential for survival: counting inventory, measuring materials, telling time, understanding seasons, dividing resources fairly, and building structures. This curriculum grounds every concept in physical manipulation and real-world application before introducing abstract notation.

## Year 1 (Kindergarten Level)

### Unit 1: Counting & Number Sense (Weeks 1-12)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Stones, sticks, seeds, beans — any small countable objects
**Survival Relevance**: Counting inventory, counting people, measuring quantities

#### Learning Objectives
- Rote count to 50
- Count objects accurately to 20 (one-to-one correspondence)
- Recognize and write numerals 0-20
- Understand "more than," "less than," "equal to"
- Count forward from any number within 20

#### Teacher Notes
Use REAL objects always. Never start with worksheets. Children count seeds into bags, count people at meals, count steps to the water source. Math is not a school subject — it is a life tool. Make that visible.

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-3: Counting to 10**
- Activity: Counting collections — give each child a pile of 5-10 objects. "How many do you have?" Touch each one while counting. Line them up to verify.
- Practice: Count fingers, toes, people in the room, trees outside, stones in a pile
- Key concept: One-to-one correspondence — one touch, one count, one number

**Weeks 4-6: Numerals 0-10**
- Activity: Match numeral cards to object groups. Write numerals in sand/dirt.
- Practice: "Show me 7 stones." "Write the number that tells how many."
- Key concept: A numeral is a symbol that represents a quantity

**Weeks 7-9: Counting to 20**
- Activity: Extend counting with larger collections. Introduce teen numbers as "ten and some more" (13 = ten and three more)
- Practice: Count items into groups of 10 + extras

**Weeks 10-12: Comparing Quantities**
- Activity: Two piles of objects. "Which has more? Which has fewer? Are they equal?" Line up side-by-side to compare.
- Vocabulary: More, fewer/less, equal, same, greater, most, fewest

#### Assessment
- Counts to 50 orally without errors
- Counts 20 objects accurately
- Writes numerals 0-20
- Correctly compares two groups as more/less/equal

### Unit 2: Addition & Subtraction within 10 (Weeks 13-26)
**Duration**: 14 weeks
**Materials**: Countable objects, drawings
**Survival Relevance**: Combining supplies, sharing resources, figuring out shortages

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand addition as "putting together" and "adding to"
- Understand subtraction as "taking away" and "taking apart"
- Solve addition and subtraction problems within 10
- Use objects, fingers, and drawings to solve
- Write number sentences (3 + 2 = 5, 7 - 4 = 3)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 13-16: Addition Concept**
- Story problems: "You have 3 apples. I give you 2 more. How many now?" Act it out with real objects.
- Activity: Combine groups. Count the total. Record as number sentence.
- Practice: Addition within 5, then extend to within 10

**Weeks 17-20: Subtraction Concept**
- Story problems: "You have 8 berries. You eat 3. How many left?" Act it out.
- Activity: Start with a group, remove some, count what remains
- Practice: Subtraction within 5, then within 10

**Weeks 21-24: Fact Fluency**
- Activity: Practice addition and subtraction facts with games — dice rolling, card games, dominoes
- Target: Know all facts within 5 automatically (no counting needed)

**Weeks 25-26: Word Problems**
- Activity: Solve story problems without objects, using drawings or mental math
- Types: Join (adding to), Separate (taking from), Part-Part-Whole, Compare

### Unit 3: Shapes, Patterns & Measurement (Weeks 27-36)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: String, sticks, stones, containers, water
**Survival Relevance**: Construction requires understanding shapes and measurement. Patterns are the foundation of scientific thinking.

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify and name: circle, square, rectangle, triangle, sphere, cube, cylinder
- Describe shapes using attributes (sides, corners, flat, curved)
- Create, extend, and describe repeating patterns (AB, ABB, ABC)
- Compare lengths, weights, and capacities using direct comparison
- Use non-standard units to measure (hand spans, footsteps, cups)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 27-29: 2D and 3D Shapes**
- Activity: Shape hunt — find circles, squares, triangles in the environment. Build shapes with sticks and string.
- Practice: Sort objects by shape, describe attributes

**Weeks 30-32: Patterns**
- Activity: Clap patterns, bead/stone patterns, color patterns, movement patterns
- Practice: "What comes next?" Create your own pattern for a partner to extend.
- Survival link: Seasons are a pattern. Day/night is a pattern. Planting cycles are patterns.

**Weeks 33-36: Measurement**
- Activity: "Which stick is longer? How do we know?" Direct comparison, then measuring with non-standard units.
- Practice: Measure the garden bed in footsteps. Measure water in cups. Compare weights on a balance (stick over a fulcrum).

---

### Unit 4: Time & Data (Weeks 37-40)
**Duration**: 4 weeks
**Materials**: Sundial (stick in ground), tally marks, simple charts
**Survival Relevance**: Timekeeping for schedules, watch shifts, planting; data collection for inventory and weather tracking

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand sequence: morning, afternoon, evening, night
- Read a simple sundial for approximate time of day
- Understand days of the week and their sequence
- Collect and organize simple data (tally marks)
- Read simple picture graphs and bar graphs

#### Lessons
**Weeks 37-38: Time Concepts**
- Activity: Daily schedule — what do we do in the morning? Afternoon? Track sunrise/sunset.
- Practice: Sequence daily events. Learn days of the week through songs/chants.

**Weeks 39-40: Data Collection**
- Activity: Survey classmates (favorite food, favorite animal). Record with tally marks. Make picture graph.
- Survival link: "How many eggs did the chickens lay this week?" Tally each day, make a graph.

## Year 2 (1st Grade Level)

### Unit 5: Place Value — Tens and Ones (Weeks 1-10)
**Duration**: 10 weeks
**Materials**: Bundles of 10 sticks, loose sticks, beans in cups of 10
**Survival Relevance**: Understanding large numbers for inventory management, resource counting

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand that 10 ones = 1 ten
- Represent numbers 11-99 as tens and ones
- Read, write, and compare numbers to 100
- Count by 10s to 100, count by 5s to 50, count by 2s to 20
- Understand number line and position of numbers

#### Lessons
**Weeks 1-4: Building Tens**
- Activity: Bundle sticks into groups of 10. "23 = 2 bundles and 3 loose sticks = 2 tens and 3 ones"
- Practice: Build numbers with physical bundles, write the numeral, say "__ tens and __ ones"

**Weeks 5-7: Reading and Writing Numbers to 100**
- Activity: Number grid to 100. Find patterns (columns all end in same digit, rows count by 10s)
- Practice: Write any number dictated, read any written number, locate on number line

**Weeks 8-10: Comparing and Skip Counting**
- Activity: Greater than/less than with two-digit numbers. Use > < = symbols.
- Practice: Skip count by 2s, 5s, and 10s (critical for multiplication foundation)

---

### Unit 6: Addition & Subtraction within 100 (Weeks 11-28)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Bundles of sticks, number line, tally marks
**Survival Relevance**: All practical math — combining supplies, calculating shortages, dividing resources

#### Learning Objectives
- Add and subtract within 20 fluently (automatic recall)
- Add and subtract two-digit numbers without regrouping (34 + 25)
- Add and subtract two-digit numbers with regrouping (38 + 47, 62 - 28)
- Solve one and two-step word problems
- Understand the relationship between addition and subtraction (fact families)

#### Lessons
**Weeks 11-16: Fluency within 20**
- Daily practice: flash card drills, games, timed challenges (not stressful — personal improvement)
- Strategies: Doubles (6+6), doubles plus one (6+7), make a ten (8+5 = 8+2+3 = 10+3)

**Weeks 17-22: Two-Digit Addition and Subtraction (No Regrouping)**
- Activity: Use sticks bundles. Add tens to tens, ones to ones. 34 + 25 = 5 tens + 9 ones = 59
- Practice: Progress from manipulatives to drawings to abstract notation

**Weeks 23-28: Regrouping**
- Activity: "38 + 47: 8 ones + 7 ones = 15 ones. That's 1 ten and 5 ones. Trade 10 ones for 1 ten bundle." Physical trading with manipulatives.
- Practice: Master regrouping in addition then subtraction. Word problems throughout.

---

### Unit 7: Measurement & Geometry Applied (Weeks 29-36)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Ruler (or marked stick), string, balance, containers
**Survival Relevance**: Building, cooking, sewing, and construction all require measurement

#### Learning Objectives
- Measure length in standard units (centimeters) using a ruler
- Measure length in non-standard units and explain why standard units matter
- Tell time to the hour and half-hour (on analog clock or sundial)
- Count coins and understand their values (if relevant to community economy)
- Identify lines of symmetry in shapes

#### Survival-Relevant Projects
- Measure garden beds and calculate how many plants fit
- Measure wood for a birdhouse or simple shelf
- Create a daily schedule using hours
- Weigh ingredients for a recipe using a balance

## Year 3 (2nd Grade Level)

### Unit 8: Place Value to 1,000 (Weeks 1-8)
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Materials**: Bundles of 100 (or drawn representations)
**Survival Relevance**: Inventory in larger communities reaches hundreds; understanding large numbers prevents errors

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand hundreds, tens, and ones
- Read, write, and compare numbers to 1,000
- Count by 100s, 50s, 25s, 10s, 5s
- Round numbers to the nearest 10 and 100
- Understand expanded form (347 = 300 + 40 + 7)

---

### Unit 9: Multi-Digit Addition & Subtraction (Weeks 9-22)
**Duration**: 14 weeks
**Materials**: Paper, writing tools
**Survival Relevance**: Managing community inventory, calculating resource needs for projects

#### Learning Objectives
- Add and subtract within 1,000 with regrouping
- Estimate sums and differences using rounding
- Solve multi-step word problems
- Check answers using inverse operations (addition checks subtraction)

---

### Unit 10: Introduction to Multiplication (Weeks 23-34)
**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Materials**: Arrays of objects (stones in rows/columns), graph paper if available
**Survival Relevance**: Calculating areas (garden plots), repeated addition (daily water needs x days), arrays (planting rows)

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand multiplication as repeated addition (3 × 4 = 4 + 4 + 4)
- Understand multiplication as arrays (3 rows of 4)
- Know multiplication facts for 0, 1, 2, 5, 10 automatically
- Begin learning 3, 4 facts
- Solve simple multiplication word problems
- Understand the commutative property (3 × 4 = 4 × 3)

#### Survival-Relevant Projects
- "We need 4 cups of water per person. There are 12 people. How many cups total?" (4 × 12)
- "Plant seeds in rows of 8. We need 6 rows. How many seeds?" (6 × 8)
- "Each chicken lays about 5 eggs per week. We have 6 chickens. How many eggs per week?" (6 × 5)
- Measure a rectangular garden: count rows and columns of planting spaces

---

### Unit 11: Measurement, Time & Money (Weeks 23-40, parallel with multiplication)
**Duration**: 18 weeks
**Materials**: Rulers, measuring cups, clocks/sundials, scale/balance
**Survival Relevance**: Every practical task in survival requires measurement

#### Learning Objectives
- Measure in centimeters and meters
- Measure in cups, liters (liquids)
- Measure weight in grams and kilograms
- Tell time to 5-minute intervals
- Understand elapsed time (started at 2:00, worked for 3 hours, finished at 5:00)
- Solve measurement word problems
- Understand perimeter as distance around a shape

#### Key Projects
- Calculate perimeter of community buildings (walk and measure)
- Measure water purification amounts accurately
- Create a daily schedule with specific times
- Calculate how much lumber needed for a project (measure and multiply)
