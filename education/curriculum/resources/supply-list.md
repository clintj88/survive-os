# Supply List — Materials by Subject and Grade Band

## Overview
This document lists all physical materials needed to teach the SURVIVE OS curriculum. Materials are organized by subject and prioritized by necessity. Where possible, natural, improvised, or community-made alternatives are noted. The curriculum is designed to function with minimal manufactured supplies.

**Priority Levels**:
- **Essential**: Cannot teach the subject without this
- **Important**: Significantly improves instruction
- **Helpful**: Nice to have, not required

---

## Universal Supplies (All Subjects, All Bands)

### Essential
- Paper (any type — lined, blank, graph; can be made from recycled materials)
- Pencils and/or charcoal sticks for writing
- Erasers
- Rulers (can be made from straight sticks with marked increments)
- Scissors (at least 1 per 5 students)
- String/twine
- Glue (can be made from flour paste, pine resin, or animal hide)
- Chalkboard or whiteboard (or flat painted surface) with chalk/markers
- Storage containers (bins, boxes, jars for organizing materials)

### Important
- Colored pencils, crayons, or natural pigments
- Tape (duct tape, masking tape)
- Notebooks or journals (can be hand-bound)
- Binder materials for student portfolios
- Timer or clock

## Mathematics

### Foundation (K-2)
- **Essential**: Counting manipulatives (stones, beans, buttons, sticks), number cards, dice
- **Important**: Base-ten blocks (can be made from wood), measuring cups/spoons, balance scale
- **Helpful**: Abacus, clock face with movable hands

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: Fraction manipulatives (can cut from paper/wood), protractor, compass (drawing), graph paper
- **Important**: Calculator (solar-powered), measuring tape, scale
- **Helpful**: Geometric solids (can be made from clay or cardboard)

### Intermediate (6-8)
- **Essential**: Scientific calculator, graph paper, straight edge, protractor, compass
- **Important**: Algebra tiles (can be made), coordinate grid boards, financial math worksheets
- **Helpful**: Surveying tools (plumb bob, level, string)

### Advanced (9-12)
- **Essential**: Scientific calculator, trigonometry tables, logarithm tables, graph paper
- **Important**: Surveying equipment (transit or theodolite if available), statistical tables
- **Helpful**: Graphing calculator, engineering scale

## Science

### Foundation (K-2)
- **Essential**: Magnifying glasses, collection jars, thermometer, rain gauge
- **Important**: Magnets, prisms, simple microscope
- **Helpful**: Binoculars, field guides

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: Magnets (various), batteries and wire (for circuits), soil test kit (pH at minimum), plant pots/containers
- **Important**: Microscope, rock and mineral samples, dissection tools
- **Helpful**: Chemistry set (basic — baking soda, vinegar, pH paper), telescope

### Intermediate (6-8)
- **Essential**: Microscope, slides, dissection kit, pH paper, water testing supplies, weather instruments (thermometer, barometer, rain gauge, wind gauge)
- **Important**: Chemistry supplies (see chemistry section), biological specimen collection supplies, star charts
- **Helpful**: Telescope, laboratory glassware

### Advanced (9-12)
- **Essential**: Laboratory glassware (beakers, flasks, test tubes, graduated cylinders), balance/scale (accurate to 0.1g), pH meter or paper, thermometers, microscope with multiple objectives
- **Important**: Distillation apparatus, spectroscope (can be made from CD), centrifuge (hand-cranked), autoclave or pressure cooker for sterilization
- **Helpful**: Analytical balance, laboratory-grade chemicals, chromatography supplies

## Health & Medicine

### Foundation (K-2)
- **Essential**: Soap, bandages (fabric strips work), basic hygiene supplies for demonstration
- **Important**: Anatomy poster or model, food group visual aids
- **Helpful**: Toothbrush models, hand-washing timer

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: First aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, tape, scissors, gloves), CPR training cushion
- **Important**: Puberty education materials (age-appropriate diagrams), nutrition planning worksheets
- **Helpful**: Blood pressure cuff for demonstration, anatomy model

### Intermediate (6-8)
- **Essential**: First aid supplies (suture practice materials — banana peels or silicone pads, splinting materials, wound packing materials), CPR mannequin, triage tags
- **Important**: Anatomy references, microscope for pathogen education, water testing supplies
- **Helpful**: Stethoscope, otoscope, diagnostic equipment for training

### Advanced (9-12)
- **Essential**: Complete medical training supplies (suture kits, splinting materials, airway equipment, blood pressure cuffs, stethoscopes), pharmacology references, anatomy atlas
- **Important**: Obstetric training model, dental instruments for training, IV training arms
- **Helpful**: Ultrasound (if available), laboratory diagnostic equipment

- **Important**: Magnifying glasses for insect observation, plant identification cards
- **Helpful**: Rain gauge, soil thermometer

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: Garden plot access, full-size garden tools, seeds (diverse varieties), soil testing kit, composting area, chicken coop access
- **Important**: Seed saving supplies (envelopes, desiccant), irrigation materials (hose, watering can, drip supplies)

- **Essential**: Agricultural land access, livestock access, soil chemistry testing supplies, irrigation system components, food preservation equipment (drying racks, fermentation vessels, smoking setup)

- **Essential**: Farm-scale production area, breeding records and pedigree forms, soil laboratory equipment, water quality testing, complete food processing facility, economic planning tools
- **Important**: Surveying equipment for water management, greenhouse, aquaponics components
- **Helpful**: Soil spectrophotometer, microscope for soil biology, weather station

## Engineering & Trades

### Foundation (K-2)
- **Essential**: Child-safe scissors, glue, tape, string, basic building materials (sticks, clay, cardboard)
- **Important**: Simple tools (plastic or child-sized hammer, screwdriver)
- **Helpful**: LEGO or building blocks

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: Hand tools (saw, hammer, hand drill, screwdrivers, chisels, files), measuring tape, square, nails/screws, wood
- **Important**: Sewing supplies (needles, thread, fabric), leather working basics (punch, needle), fire-starting supplies
- **Helpful**: Soldering iron (for electrical circuits), clamps, vise

### Intermediate (6-8)
- **Essential**: Full workshop hand tool set, technical drawing tools (T-square, triangles, compass, scale ruler), metalworking basics (files, hacksaw, vise), plumbing supplies (pipe, fittings, wrenches), electrical supplies (wire, switches, outlets, boxes)
- **Important**: Forge or metal heating setup, masonry tools, small engine for instruction
- **Helpful**: Welding equipment, lathe, drill press

### Advanced (9-12)
- **Essential**: Complete workshop (woodworking, metalworking, electrical), drafting equipment, construction tools, survey equipment, welding/brazing/soldering equipment
- **Important**: Solar panels, batteries, charge controllers, inverters for power system training, radio and electronic components
- **Helpful**: Machine tools (lathe, drill press, milling machine), oscilloscope, multimeter

## Communication Technology

### Foundation (K-2)
- **Essential**: None specific — communication concepts taught with voice, signals, drawing

### Building (3-5)
- **Essential**: Morse code reference charts, flag or signal materials, battery/wire/bulb for circuits
- **Important**: Meshtastic device for demonstration
- **Helpful**: Simple radio receiver (crystal radio kit)

### Intermediate (6-8)
- **Essential**: Meshtastic devices (at least 2), basic electronics components (breadboard, resistors, capacitors, LEDs, wire), soldering equipment, ham radio (VHF/UHF)
- **Important**: Antenna materials, multimeter, Morse code key
- **Helpful**: Computer for programming instruction, oscilloscope

### Advanced (9-12)
- **Essential**: Ham radio equipment (HF, VHF, UHF), Meshtastic nodes (multiple), electronics lab (oscilloscope, multimeter, signal generator, components), antenna building materials, computer with Python
- **Important**: Network equipment (switches, cables, crimpers), solar power supplies for radio equipment
- **Helpful**: Software-defined radio, spectrum analyzer

## Improvised Material Alternatives

| Manufactured Item | Natural/Improvised Alternative |
|---|---|
| Paper | Birch bark, cloth, homemade paper (from plant fiber) |
| Pencils | Charcoal sticks, graphite chunks, sharpened slate |
| Ink | Walnut hull ink, berry juice, soot + water + gum arabic |
| Glue | Flour paste, pine pitch, hide glue |
| Paint | Berry pigments, clay slip, charcoal + binder |
| Rulers | Marked straight sticks |
| Compass (drawing) | String + pencil + nail |
| Protractor | Marked semicircle on wood or cardboard |
| Counting manipulatives | Stones, acorns, beans, buttons |
| Measuring cups | Marked containers (calibrate with known volume) |
| Notebooks | Hand-bound pages (folded + stitched) |
| Whiteboard | Painted plywood with dry-erase marker or chalk paint |
| Magnifying glass | Water droplet on glass, polished clear stone |
| Level | Water in clear tubing |
| Plumb bob | String + heavy object |
