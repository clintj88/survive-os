# Book Recommendations — Prioritized Salvage and Preservation List

## Overview
This is a prioritized list of books to salvage, preserve, and use for educational purposes. Books are organized by subject with explanations of why each is valuable. In a post-collapse scenario, books are irreplaceable knowledge stores. Prioritize salvaging based on community needs.

**Priority Levels**:
- **Critical**: Salvage first. Direct survival and educational value.
- **High**: Salvage when possible. Significant educational or reference value.
- **Standard**: Preserve if found. Enriches the curriculum.

---

## General References

### Critical
- **A comprehensive encyclopedia set** (Britannica, World Book, or equivalent) — the single most valuable reference for a post-collapse community. Covers every subject at accessible depth.
- **A complete dictionary** (unabridged if possible) — language reference, vocabulary building, spelling
- **A world atlas** — geography, navigation, climate zones, resources
- **Pocket Ref by Thomas Glover** — incredible density of practical reference data (conversions, formulas, specifications)
- **The Knowledge: How to Rebuild Civilization in the Aftermath of a Cataclysm by Lewis Dartnell** — exactly our scenario. Covers agriculture, materials, medicine, power, transport, communication, chemistry

### High
- **Marks' Standard Handbook for Mechanical Engineers** — comprehensive engineering reference
- **The Merck Manual of Diagnosis and Therapy** — medical reference (see Medicine section)
- **CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics** — fundamental science reference
- **Machinery's Handbook** — machining, metalworking, engineering reference

## Preservation Guidelines

### Priority Actions
1. **Protect from water** — books die from moisture faster than anything else
2. **Control temperature** — cool, stable temperatures extend book life dramatically
3. **Control light** — UV degrades paper and binding. Store in dark when not in use.
4. **Protect from pests** — insects and rodents destroy books. Sealed storage, regular inspection.
5. **Handle carefully** — clean hands, support spine, don't force bindings open flat

### Long-Term Preservation
- Copy critical information by hand if books are deteriorating
- Prioritize copying content that exists in only one copy
- Consider creating community-made copies of the most critical references
- If printing capability exists, reproduce key references
- Digital backup on SURVIVE OS systems where available

### Community Library Organization
- Catalog all books (title, author, subject, condition, location)
- Designate a librarian responsible for collection management
- Establish checkout and return procedures
- Prioritize repair of damaged books (spine repair, page mending)
- Regular inventory to prevent loss
